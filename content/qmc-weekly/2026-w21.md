_A quiet week on arXiv but a substantive one for journals: a transformer-style neural ansatz landed in PRR, a sign-problem post-mortem from the Scalettar group appeared in PRB, and two complementary takes on RNN-based NQS and ML exchange–correlation showed up on arXiv. The throughline: people are still asking how much architecture choice actually buys you over a well-trained baseline._

## Highlights

- **PSR-NQS (arXiv:2605.13807)** revives recurrent NQS via parallel-scan recurrences and pushes 2D spin lattices to $52\times52$ — a useful counterpoint to the "everything must be a transformer" trend.
- **Convolutional transformer wave functions (PRR)** is the journal-version statement of the opposite bet: convolutional inductive bias inside an attention stack.
- **Larson–Mondaini–Scalettar (PRB)** finally writes down a careful sign-resolved analysis of QMC bias, which neural-VMC people who quote "we beat AFQMC by X mHa" should probably read.

## Papers

### Parallel Scan Recurrent Neural Quantum States for Scalable Variational Monte Carlo
_Merali, Hibat-Allah, Kohandel, Scalettar, Khatami_ · [arXiv:2605.13807](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13807v1) · cond-mat.str-el

Argues that recurrent NQS aren't intrinsically un-scalable: by replacing serial RNN evaluation with a parallel-scan formulation, autoregressive recurrent wave functions become tractable for VMC on 2D lattices up to $52\times52$, with iterative retraining used to climb to larger system sizes while staying consistent with reference QMC. The result is a useful sanity check that "RNN ⇒ slow" is an implementation artifact, not an architectural fact, and that the transformer-vs-RNN gap may be narrower than recent NQS literature suggests once you fix the parallelism story.

### Expander attention as exchange-correlation
_Alaa El-Din, v. Strachwitz, Vinko_ · [arXiv:2605.10265](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.10265v1) · quant-ph

Proposes a linearly-scaling non-local ML exchange–correlation functional built on an expander-graph transformer ansatz, motivated by the cost wall faced by previous ML-XC schemes ($O(N^2)$ or worse) that did reach strong correlation. They recover the H$_2$ dissociation curve in the strongly correlated regime and report encouraging results on planar H$_4$, a benchmark where coupled cluster wobbles. It's DFT rather than VMC, but the antisymmetry-via-attention question is the same one neural-VMC keeps revisiting, so worth tracking.

### Convolutional transformer wave functions
_Anonymous (PRR listing)_ · [Phys. Rev. Research](https://doi.org/10.1103/7xwp-25y9) · Physical Review Research

Journal version of the convolutional-attention hybrid NQS thread. The setup is the natural one: replace pure-MLP token mixers in transformer wave functions with convolutions to bake in translation symmetry and locality, then keep the attention for long-range correlations. Without the PRR text in hand it's hard to call the numerical claims, but the design is squarely in the "give the transformer a physics prior" pattern that has been steadily eating performance from generic-architecture NQS.

### Sign-resolved statistics and the origin of bias in quantum Monte Carlo
_Larson, Mondaini, Scalettar_ · [Phys. Rev. B](https://doi.org/10.1103/x24t-bd2q) · Physical Review B

A close look at where bias actually enters sign-problem-afflicted QMC, by separating the positive- and negative-weight sectors and tracking how each contributes to estimators. This is the kind of paper neural-VMC results should be benchmarked against more honestly: when an NQS reports lower energy than constrained-path AFQMC, knowing whether the AFQMC bias is sign-pinning vs. trial-state choice matters a lot for the interpretation.

### Simulating non-Markovian open quantum dynamics by exploiting physics-informed neural network
_Cao, Ge, Zhang, Wang, Xu, Yan, Zheng_ · [J. Chem. Phys.](https://doi.org/10.1063/5.0332977) · The Journal of Chemical Physics

Embeds a PINN inside a neural quantum state to integrate the dissipaton-embedded quantum master equation (DQME), avoiding the time-dependent variational principle step. Benchmarks against numerically exact HEOM on the single-impurity Anderson model are accurate in the high-T (weakly non-Markovian) regime, but the authors explicitly flag error accumulation under strong non-Markovianity at low T. A clean, refreshingly honest write-up of what time-domain PINNs can and can't do for dissipative dynamics.

### A staging Monte Carlo algorithm for sampling off-diagonal density matrix elements via open-chain path integrals
_Robledo, Tuckerman_ · [J. Chem. Phys.](https://doi.org/10.1063/5.0331949) · The Journal of Chemical Physics

Not neural, but a clean methodological piece: an open-chain PIMC sampler that extends the Pollock–Ceperley staging trick to off-diagonal density-matrix elements, giving direct access to momentum distributions. The algorithm is Gaussian sampling + a Metropolis step on top of a recursion, and reproduces end-to-end and momentum distributions from coupled oscillators up to liquid water. Worth bookmarking if you ever want momentum-resolved observables out of an NQS-augmented path integral.

## Trends & Themes

Two architectural bets sitting next to each other this week — parallel-scan RNNs (PSR-NQS) on one side, convolutional-transformer wave functions on the other — and both are essentially arguing that the bottleneck isn't "which sequence model" but "which physics prior you bake in and how you parallelize it." Meanwhile the Scalettar-group QMC-bias paper is a useful reminder that the reference points neural-VMC papers compare to are themselves biased in structured ways, which should reshape how "we beat method X by Y mHa" claims get read.
