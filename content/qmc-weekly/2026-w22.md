_A quiet week in core neural-VMC for electrons, but neural ansätze showed up in unusual venues: variational Monte Carlo applied to quantum reduced loop gravity, neural canonical transformations for the spectra of fluxional CH$_5^+$, and a transformer that learns the self-energy operator for strongly correlated systems. Worth a look even if you're not in the FermiNet/PauliNet trenches this week._

## Highlights

- Mäkinen, Sahlmann & Sherif push neural quantum states into **quantum reduced loop gravity**, using VMC on the symmetric Hamiltonian's $\hat{\mathcal Q} = \hat C \hat C^\dagger$ to map out the near-kernel sector — and recover Thiemann-style coherent states as an emergent semiclassical branch.
- Wang, Zhang & Wang extend the **neural canonical transformation** (NCT) framework to excited vibrational states of the famously floppy CH$_5^+$ molecule, working directly in atomic (not normal-mode) coordinates.
- A new PRB paper proposes **transformer-based operator learning for the self-energy** in strongly correlated systems — an interesting bridge between neural ansatz literature and many-body Green's function methods.

## Papers

### Emergent Thiemann coherent states in the near-kernel sector of quantum reduced loop gravity
_Ilkka Mäkinen, Hanno Sahlmann, Waleed Sherif_ · [arXiv:2605.18625](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18625v1) · gr-qc

The authors use VMC with **neural quantum states** on the one-vertex model of quantum reduced loop gravity, variationally minimizing the positive operator $\hat{\mathcal Q} = \hat C \hat C^\dagger$ (built from Euclidean + Lorentzian contributions to the Hamiltonian constraint) in truncated Hilbert spaces with spin cutoff up to $j_{\max} = 1001$. They find the near-kernel states organize into three classes: a non-factorizing low-cutoff solution, and two factorized branches at larger cutoffs. One branch is reproduced with near-unit fidelity by reduced Thiemann coherent states — concrete numerical evidence for an emergent semiclassical structure that LQG people have long argued about analytically. The other factorized branch is just as clean, but lives outside the Thiemann coherent-state family, which raises an honest question about what it actually represents. Either way, this is a nice demo that NQS-based VMC has reach well outside electronic structure.

### Neural canonical transformation for the spectra of fluxional molecule CH$_5^+$
_Ruisi Wang, Qi Zhang, Lei Wang_ · [Mach. Learn.: Sci. Technol.](https://doi.org/10.1088/2632-2153/ae6caf) · Machine Learning: Science and Technology

Protonated methane is a torture test: large-amplitude hydrogen motion makes normal-coordinate expansions of the vibrational wavefunction essentially useless. The authors apply their **neural canonical transformation** (a variational ansatz combining a normalizing flow over coordinates with a base wavefunction) directly in Cartesian/atomic coordinates, computing ground and excited vibrational states. The resulting wavefunctions show clear preferences for the three stationary points on the PES, with low- and high-energy excited states distributed differently across them. The result extends NCT — previously demonstrated on molecules with fixed-ish geometry and on solids — into the genuinely fluxional regime, which is where classical VSCF/VCI methods start to die. Worth comparing to diffusion Monte Carlo and MCTDH results on the same system; the paper does this.

### Transformer-based operator learning framework for self-energy in strongly correlated systems
_Anonymous (PRB submission)_ · [Phys. Rev. B](https://doi.org/10.1103/k5s2-x6hq) · Physical Review B

A transformer is trained as an operator-learning surrogate that maps an input (presumably some combination of bath/hybridization function and interaction parameters) to the impurity self-energy $\Sigma(\omega)$ for strongly correlated impurity problems. With no abstract publicly available it's hard to judge the architectural details or benchmarks against ED/CTQMC, but the framing — using attention to learn a functional on frequency-dependent objects — is in the same neighborhood as recent work on neural Green's-function surrogates for DMFT. If the training set is honest and the held-out regime is non-trivial, this could be a useful drop-in for embedding-style workflows that need a fast (but reliable) self-energy. Skeptical until I see the data; flagged for follow-up when the preprint surfaces.

### Fast and accurate committor estimation for kinetics simulations
_Ru Wang, Xiaojun Ji, Hao Wang, Wenjian Liu_ · [arXiv:2605.17753](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.17753v1) · physics.chem-ph

A bit off the core neural-VMC beat — this is **classical** kinetics, not quantum VMC — but the structure is the same: parameterize a scalar field (here the committor $q(\mathbf{x})$, the optimal reaction coordinate) with a **neural network ansatz**, train it from short trajectories + analogue prediction, then couple to Milestoning for mean first-passage times. Included because the neural-ansatz toolkit for reaction-coordinate problems is converging on the same tricks as neural-VMC (variational loss on a scalar field, importance sampling from short trajectories), and cross-pollination is worth tracking.

## Trends & Themes

Two of this week's most substantive papers (LQG near-kernel sector; CH$_5^+$ vibrational spectra) share a pattern: **take a neural variational ansatz, apply it to a quantum problem where conventional basis-set expansions are hopeless**. That's arguably where neural ansätze offer the most defensible value-add — not "shave $1$ mHa off the H$_2$O ground state" but "solve a problem you literally cannot tile with a product basis." The transformer self-energy paper points at a different but related direction: neural surrogates inside many-body workflows, learning functionals rather than wavefunctions. Both threads are worth watching.
