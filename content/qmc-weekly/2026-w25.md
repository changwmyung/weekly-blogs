_A quiet week with one standout: a continuum neural ansatz that is a momentum eigenstate **by construction**, used to map out phases of 2D bosons in a single sweep. On the journal side, a physics-inspired transformer NQS trained via latent imaginary-time evolution and a careful look at variational entanglement Hamiltonians round things out._

## Highlights

- **EVE (Dai & Soljačić)** builds total-momentum eigenstates into a neural quantum state for continuum particles — superfluid, roton, crystal, and phonon all fall out of one ansatz at different densities.
- **Latent imaginary-time evolution** for transformer NQS — instead of variational energy minimization, the network is trained to mimic $e^{-\tau H}$ in a learned latent space.
- **Variational entanglement Hamiltonians** get a quadrature-based cost reformulation that cuts measurement budgets by >10×, with honest caveats about what a low cost value does and doesn't certify.

## Papers

### Continuum Neural Momentum Eigenstate for Variationally Solving Quasiparticles
_Dai, Soljačić_ · [arXiv:2606.12928](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12928v1) · cond-mat.quant-gas

EVE is a continuum neural quantum state engineered so that, for any chosen momentum $\mathbf{k}$, the wave function is **exactly** an eigenstate of total momentum with eigenvalue $\mathbf{k}$ — no Lagrange multipliers, no soft penalty, no projection on top. That turns off-the-shelf VMC into a tool for momentum-resolved spectra. They test it on 2D bosons with $1/r$ interactions and recover four qualitatively different states from one architecture: at $r_s=20$ the dispersion shows the textbook superfluid roton minimum, at $r_s=100$ they see zone-folded crystalline order with phonon arcs between the floating-crystal minima. The phase-texture analysis of the roton even surfaces multi-particle phase strings from merged vortex dipoles — exactly the kind of structural insight that's hard to get from a generic NQS without a baked-in symmetry.

### Physics-inspired transformer quantum states via latent imaginary-time evolution
_Anonymous (PRR accepted)_ · [Phys. Rev. Research 10.1103/bjxb-8tsk](https://doi.org/10.1103/bjxb-8tsk) · Physical Review Research

Transformer-style NQS continue to spread; this one trains the network to approximate imaginary-time evolution $\psi \mapsto e^{-\tau H}\psi$ in a learned latent space rather than minimizing the variational energy directly. The pitch is improved expressivity at fixed parameter count and a smoother optimization landscape — both old pain points for transformer ansätze on frustrated lattice problems. Worth reading alongside the recent PsiFormer-style work to see whether latent-space evolution generalizes beyond the benchmark Hamiltonians.

### Exploring Variational Entanglement Hamiltonians
_Kind, Fauseweh_ · [Quantum **10**, 2133 (2026)](https://doi.org/10.22331/q-2026-06-12-2133) · Quantum

Not pure neural-VMC, but methodologically adjacent and a useful read for anyone variationally targeting non-energy cost functions. They reinterpret the entanglement-Hamiltonian cost functional as an integral, deploy iterative quadrature, and cut required measurements by an order of magnitude even with noise. A modified ansatz captures deviations from the Bisognano–Wichmann form in lattice models and doubles as a phase-transition diagnostic. The honest punchline: a low cost value does **not** guarantee small trace distance — but degeneracies and spectral gaps are reproduced faithfully, which is what topological-phase applications actually need.

## Trends & Themes

Two of three picks this week are about **baking structure into the ansatz** (momentum quantum numbers in EVE, latent-space imaginary-time dynamics in the transformer NQS) rather than throwing more parameters at a generic network. The third is a reminder that **what you minimize is not always what you measure** — a theme that keeps recurring as VMC-style methods get pushed into non-energy targets. Quiet week, but the EVE paper is the kind of architectural move worth tracking: symmetry-by-construction beats symmetry-by-penalty every time you can get away with it.
