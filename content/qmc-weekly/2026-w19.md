_A quiet but methodologically focused week. Three arXiv preprints landed, all of them touching the **infrastructure** of neural quantum states rather than headline applications: a bosonic grand-canonical ansatz, the first NQS treatment of open quantum-optical many-body dynamics, and an overdue fix for the estimation bias in time-dependent VMC._

## Highlights

- **Time-dependent VMC has had a hidden bias all along.** A new unbiased formulation via self-normalized importance sampling on a cutoff-deformed Born distribution removes it. Anyone running long-time real-time NN-VMC should care.
- **NQS finally enters open-system quantum optics.** First neural ansatz beating exact / tensor-network methods on light-matter-coupled dissipative many-body dynamics — opens the door to NQS for cavity QED and waveguide QED problems where structured dissipation breaks classical scaling.
- **Bosonic NQS in Fock space, variable particle number.** Grand-canonical bosonic ansatz: variable-N statistical mechanics now within neural-VMC reach.

## Papers

### Neural network quantum states in the grand canonical ensemble
_authors et al._ · [arXiv:2605.07779](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.07779) · quant-ph · 2026-05-08
Proposes an NQS architecture for symmetric bosonic wavefunctions in Fock space, supporting variable particle number. Combined with VMC sampling and geometric optimization, achieves competitive variational energies across a benchmark suite. Opens NN-VMC to grand-canonical statistical mechanics, where prior fixed-N tricks were either approximate or expensive.

### Neural network modeling of many-body super- and sub-radiant dynamics
_authors et al._ · [arXiv:2605.04640](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04640) · quant-ph · 2026-05-06
First application of NQS to dissipative dynamics of structured light-matter interfaces — super- and sub-radiance in long-range-interacting open systems. Operates beyond the reach of exact and tensor-network methods. The methodological transplant of NQS from closed-system Hamiltonians to open quantum-optical many-body problems is a meaningful expansion of the toolkit.

### Time-dependent variational Monte Carlo without bias
_authors et al._ · [arXiv:2605.03930](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03930) · quant-ph · 2026-05-05
Identifies and fixes a subtle estimation bias in traditional TDVMC. Two avenues: self-normalized importance sampling against a cutoff-deformed Born distribution, plus an alternative variant. Particularly important at long times and high entanglement, where the bias compounds. If you've been running TD NN-VMC for dynamics, your previous numbers may have been quietly contaminated.

## Trends & Themes

A methods-heavy week: open systems, variable-N, and a bias-correction infrastructure paper. None of these is the next FermiNet — but the cumulative effect is that the **TDVMC + NN-ansatz toolkit will be more reliable next week than last week**, which matters more for the next application paper than another mHa on benzene would.
