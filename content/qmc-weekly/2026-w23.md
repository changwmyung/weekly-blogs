_Quiet week. Two arXiv hits clear the bar — one a long-overdue benchmark for neural wavefunction expressivity, the other a structured-QST review that brushes against neural quantum states. Crossref returned nothing from our journal list. We keep it short._

## Highlights

- **WF-Bench** lands the first systematic, fidelity-based benchmark for neural-network wavefunctions, with empirical scaling laws over depth and determinant count on Psiformer/FermiNet.
- A review on **compressive QST** stitches neural quantum states into the broader story of low-rank / tensor-network / shallow-circuit state reconstruction — useful as a map of the territory rather than a new result.

## Papers

### WF-Bench: A Benchmark for Neural Network WaveFunction Expressivity and Scaling Laws
_Zhang, Duan, Luo_ · [arXiv:2605.29683](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.29683v1) · physics.comp-ph

A benchmark dataset that asks neural ansätze to *match* known many-body target wavefunctions — topological states, Wigner crystals, superconducting wavefunctions — and scores them by fidelity rather than the usual variational energy. The authors apply the protocol to FermiNet and Psiformer and extract empirical scaling laws in network depth and number of determinants. This is the kind of infrastructure paper neural-VMC has been missing: variational energy alone is a notoriously slippery yardstick (you can sometimes win mHa while missing the relevant order parameter), and fidelity-to-target gives a cleaner answer about *what the network can actually represent*. Worth watching whether the community adopts it.

### Statistical and Algorithmic Foundations of Probing Quantum Systems with Compressive Measurements: A Review
_Qin, Wakin, Zhu_ · [arXiv:2605.27191](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.27191v1) · quant-ph

A review of structured quantum state tomography that unifies low-rank, tensor-network, shallow-circuit, and neural quantum state ansätze under one compressive-sensing lens, then surveys measurement design (informationally complete POVMs, randomized measurements) and reconstruction algorithms. The neural-quantum-state content is not the central thrust, but the framing — sample complexity bounds on structured priors — is the right setting for thinking about *learning* neural ansätze from data rather than from energy minimization. Useful as background; not a result paper.

## Trends & Themes

If there's a theme this week, it's **evaluation infrastructure**: WF-Bench on the ansatz side, the QST review on the measurement side. Both try to replace "lower energy / smaller error" headlines with quantitative statements about *representability* and *sample complexity*. Neural-VMC is overdue for this kind of discipline; one quiet week of meta-work is fine.
