_The first four months of 2026 mostly weren't about chasing another mHa on benzene. They were about **understanding why neural quantum states (NQS) work as well as they do**, **fixing structural deficiencies** (spin symmetry, sampling bias, scalability bottlenecks), and **a serious push into application physics** — from fractional quantum Hall crystals to bilayer-nickelate superconductivity to nuclear structure. Foundation-model-style ideas also began appearing in the QMC world. This YTD digest gathers 17 representative arXiv preprints from January through early May 2026, organized by theme._

---

## TL;DR

- **Architecture year-of-the-backflow:** convolutional/spin-adapted/Pareto-optimal backflow ansätze (SCALE, ACE, SA-NNBF) replace pure mean-field-style FermiNet/PauliNet pieces with neural-modified Slater-Pfaffian determinants. Transformer ansätze gain rigorous physics framing (PITQS, Transformer NQS for spin+fermion lattices).
- **Theory finally arrives:** Walsh-complexity bounds on shallow additive NQS, autoregressive information-theoretic scaling laws (NQS analogue of LLM scaling laws), and a clean characterization of the NQS loss landscape (essentially no energy barriers between independent minima).
- **Foundation model arrives:** **Neural Operator Quantum State (NOQS)** learns the time-evolution *operator* itself — one trained model handles arbitrary driving protocols.
- **Real-time dynamics scales to 3D, 1000 qubits.** Kibble-Zurek in 3D transverse-field Ising captured by NQS without breaking.
- **Applications jump in ambition:** FQH crystals with disorder, mixD bilayer nickelate superconductivity, atomic nuclei in non-stabilizer regimes, ab-initio solids via neural backflow.

## 1. Architectures: the year of the smart backflow

### Pareto Frontier of Neural Quantum States: Scalable, Affordable, and Accurate Convolutional Backflow for Strongly Correlated Lattice Fermions
[arXiv:2604.25775](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25775) · cond-mat.str-el · 2026-04-28
Introduces two architectures — **SCALE** (efficient sparse convolutional ansatz) and **ACE** (state-of-the-art accuracy) — and benchmarks them as opposite ends of a Pareto frontier for Hubbard and t-J models on large lattices. SCALE makes NQS calculations affordable for routine condensed-matter use, ACE pushes accuracy past prior NQS baselines. The "pick your operating point" framing is the right way to think about NQS deployment for the next year.

### Spin-adapted neural network backflow for strongly correlated electrons
[arXiv:2604.06841](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06841) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-04-08
**SA-NNBF** enforces total-spin symmetry by construction via a neural backflow × spin-eigenfunction sum-of-products. Spin contamination has been a quiet failure mode in NN-VMC for transition metal complexes; this fixes it cleanly without the typical penalty term hacks. The right kind of symmetry-by-construction.

### Excited Pfaffians: Generalized Neural Wave Functions Across Structure and State
[arXiv:2603.14515](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.14515) · cs.LG · 2026-03-15
Multi-state importance sampling (MSIS) + a single Excited-Pfaffian architecture represent multiple excited states at near-constant sample cost. Solves the previously linear scaling of pairwise overlap evaluation across excited states — the key bottleneck for systematic excited-state NN-VMC.

### Transformer Neural-Network Quantum States for lattice models of spins and fermions
[arXiv:2603.02316](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.02316) · cond-mat.str-el · 2026-03-02
Tokenized transformer NQS handle composite spin+fermion local Hilbert spaces; orbitals are predicted via attention as a backflow. Applied to the 1D Ancilla Layer Model. The transplant of NLP tokenization into lattice QM is now clean enough to be templatable.

### Physics-inspired transformer quantum states via latent imaginary-time evolution
[arXiv:2602.03031](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.03031) · cond-mat.dis-nn · 2026-02-03
Reinterprets transformer NQS as approximate latent imaginary-time evolution, then enforces a static effective Hamiltonian via weight-sharing across layers. The resulting **PITQS** improves accuracy and is the kind of physics-motivated transparency the field needs to stop treating these as black boxes.

### Taming the expressiveness of neural-network wave functions
[arXiv:2603.15853](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.15853) · cond-mat.supr-con · 2026-03-16
Addresses the over-expressivity / under-convergence trade-off that has plagued NN wavefunctions on strongly correlated many-body states. Regularization schemes that stabilize optimization without losing representational power.

## 2. Theory & scaling

### Expressibility of neural quantum states: a Walsh-complexity perspective
[arXiv:2604.03294](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.03294) · cond-mat.str-el · 2026-03-27
Introduces **Walsh complexity** (a basis-dependent measure of parity-pattern spread). Proves shallow additive NN architectures cannot generate exponentially large Walsh complexity under tame parameter scaling — meaning there are constructible states that look benign but are formally out of reach for standard shallow NQS. A concrete "what can't NQS do?" result.

### Information-Theoretic Scaling Laws of Neural Quantum States
[arXiv:2603.23468](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.23468) · quant-ph · 2026-03-24
Rigorous scaling law for autoregressive NQS: virtual-bond dimension must scale with middle-cut mutual information of the wavefunction. Provides architecture-dependent exponent predictions verified numerically across tomography, ground-state, and finite-T learning. **The NQS analogue of LLM scaling laws** — only with provable foundations rather than empirical fits.

### Essentially No Energy Barrier Between Independent Fermionic Neural Quantum State Minima
[arXiv:2601.06939](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.06939) · cond-mat.dis-nn · 2026-01-11
**Mode connectivity** for fermionic NQS: independently trained networks are connected by paths with essentially no energy barrier. The accompanying **GeoNEB** tool (stochastic reconfiguration + nudged elastic band) constructs these paths. NQS loss landscapes look closer to conventional deep learning than the field assumed — encouraging for transfer learning and warm-starting.

## 3. Sampling, dynamics, optimization

### Time-dependent variational Monte Carlo without bias
[arXiv:2605.03930](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03930) · quant-ph · 2026-05-05
Two new unbiased TDVMC formulations: self-normalized importance sampling with cutoff-deformed Born distribution, plus an alternative variant. Removes a subtle estimation bias that has been quietly contaminating real-time dynamics results — particularly visible at long times and high entanglement.

### Real-time Dynamics in 3D for up to 1000 Qubits with Neural Quantum States
[arXiv:2604.05032](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.05032) · quant-ph · 2026-04-06
Residual-based convolutional NQS scales real-time simulation to 3D transverse-field Ising on 1000 qubits. Captures fast, slow, and adiabatic quench regimes — including the **3D quantum Kibble-Zurek mechanism**. Previous 3D NQS dynamics topped out at much smaller systems; this is a significant scale jump.

### Neural network quantum states in the grand canonical ensemble
[arXiv:2605.07779](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.07779) · quant-ph · 2026-05-08
Bosonic NQS architecture representing symmetric wavefunctions in Fock space — variable particle number, geometric optimization. Opens NN-VMC to grand canonical statistical mechanics applications previously requiring fixed-N tricks.

## 4. Foundation-model direction — NOQS

### Neural Operator Quantum State: A Foundation Model for Quantum Dynamics
[arXiv:2603.25066](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25066) · quant-ph · 2026-03-26
The most ambitious 2026 H1 paper: instead of solving the Schrödinger equation per driving protocol, **NOQS learns the solution operator** that maps entire driving protocols to time-evolved states. Once trained, predicts evolution under unseen protocols without retraining. The conceptual analogue of "foundation model" finally lands in QMC — early days, but the framing is clearly correct for a field that has been re-running training per protocol.

## 5. Applications: ambitious physics catches up

### Uncovering Exotic Paired States in the 2D Spin-Imbalanced Fermi Gas with Neural Wave Functions
[arXiv:2604.24883](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24883) · cond-mat.quant-gas · 2026-04-27
AGPs-FermiNet ansatz maps zero-T phase diagram of 2D spin-imbalanced Fermi gas with short-ranged attraction — recovers FFLO in BCS limit and polarized superfluid in BEC limit, plus phase separation at strong interactions. Independent confirmation of FFLO from a purely variational neural ansatz is a notable benchmark moment.

### Crystallization in the Fractional Quantum Hall Regime with Disorder-Aware Neural Quantum States
[arXiv:2604.06316](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06316) · cond-mat.str-el · 2026-04-07
First microscopic demonstration of a disorder-pinned hole Wigner crystal near ν=2/3, explaining the reentrant integer QH effect. A **disorder-aware self-attention NQS** unifies FQH liquids and Wigner crystals in a single ansatz. Major application headline of the quarter — NQS solves a real condensed-matter puzzle, not just reproduces a benchmark.

### Neural Quantum States in Non-Stabilizer Regimes: Benchmarks with Atomic Nuclei
[arXiv:2603.28646](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.28646) · nucl-th · 2026-03-30
RBM-based NQS for medium-mass atomic nuclei in non-stabilizer regimes — testing whether NQS handle states that are simultaneously volume-law-entangled *and* non-Clifford. Useful as a benchmark beyond the usual lattice spin models; nuclear structure is a tougher proving ground than people give it credit for.

### Neural network backflow for ab-initio solid calculations
[arXiv:2603.14775](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.14775) · cond-mat.str-el · 2026-03-16
**NNBF for solids** — extends neural backflow from molecules to periodic ab-initio calculations via two-stage pruning of the configuration space. The scaling-to-solids problem is partially the configuration-space combinatorics, and physics-informed importance proxies make it tractable.

### Simulating superconductivity in mixed-dimensional t‖-J‖-J⊥ bilayers with neural quantum states
[arXiv:2602.10091](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.10091) · cond-mat.str-el · 2026-02-10
Gutzwiller-projected Hidden Fermion Pfaffian NQS on the proposed effective model for bilayer-nickelate La₃Ni₂O₇ superconductivity. Reaches 8×8×2 lattices. Identifies a crossover from tightly bound pairs to weakly bound pairs — a real prediction relevant to the active LNO debate.

## Trends & themes

A few patterns from the quarter:

- **Backflow is back.** Pure FermiNet/PauliNet-style attention-only architectures are increasingly augmented with neural backflow on top of explicit Slater/Pfaffian determinants. The combination is what's hitting Pareto fronts.
- **Symmetry-by-construction over symmetry-by-penalty.** Spin (SA-NNBF), particle number (GCNQS), and structure-vs-state generalization (Excited Pfaffians) are now enforced architecturally rather than via penalty terms. This trend will keep paying off.
- **The theory is real.** Walsh complexity, information-theoretic scaling laws, mode-connectivity loss-landscape characterizations — the field has crossed the threshold where formal results about NQS exist and are useful.
- **Foundation-model framing arrives, plausibly.** NOQS is the headline, but the "learn an operator once, deploy across protocols" pattern is going to spread.
- **Applications are ambitious enough to risk wrong answers.** FQH crystals, bilayer nickelates, atomic nuclei. These aren't benchmarks where the answer is known — NQS is being asked to make predictions. That's the real maturity test.
