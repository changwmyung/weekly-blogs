_A surprisingly meaty week for solid-state cQED: a careful negative result on superradiant phase transitions in Dirac–Landau polaritons, an ab initio collective-coupling calculation on a real porphyrin, and two theory proposals that lean on optical cavities to probe many-body physics and Jahn–Teller chemistry._

## Highlights

- Graphene Landau polaritons at $\Omega_\text{R}/\omega_\text{cav} \approx 0.4$ show **no softening of the lower polariton** — another nail in the coffin for an equilibrium superradiant phase transition in 2D Dirac systems.
- Huo's group pushes **ab initio polariton spectra to ZnTPP in the collective regime**, a useful reality check for QED-DFT-style treatments versus Tavis–Cummings phenomenology.
- A clean theory paper sketches a **cavity-QED route to the Yukawa–SYK model**, turning ultracold-atoms-in-a-cavity into a many-body-chaos simulator.

## Papers

### Absence of a Superradiant Phase Transition in Dirac Landau Polaritons
_Jöchl, Helmrich, Lindel, Hale, Graziotto, Jarrahi, Nova, Faist, Scalari_ · [arXiv:2605.27319](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.27319v1) · cond-mat.mes-hall

Encapsulated monolayer graphene ultrastrongly coupled to a single complementary split-ring THz resonator, swept by gating from weak coupling deep into USC ($\Omega_\text{R}/\omega_\text{cav} \approx 0.4$). The linear Dirac dispersion was supposed to be the cleanest loophole around the $\vec{A}^2$ No-Go theorem, so a softening of the lower polariton branch would have been the smoking gun for an SRPT. They see none — the full polariton dispersion is reproduced quantitatively by a Hopfield Hamiltonian with a careful near-field model. A welcome experimental baseline, and consistent with the broader pattern that "almost-SRPTs" keep evaporating once the cavity geometry is treated properly rather than as a single mode.

### Ab Initio Polariton Spectra of ZnTPP Molecules Collectively Coupled Inside an Optical Cavity
_Weight, Rury, Shao, Huo_ · [JACS](https://doi.org/10.1021/jacs.6c01411) · J. Am. Chem. Soc.

Huo's group extends ab initio polaritonic electronic structure beyond toy molecules to ZnTPP, a real porphyrin chromophore, in the collective (many-molecule) coupling regime relevant to actual cavity experiments. The interesting question is always how much of the rich Tavis–Cummings phenomenology survives once you have a realistic molecular Hamiltonian with vibronic structure and disorder. This is the kind of calibration paper polaritonic chemistry needs — without it, claims about "cavity-modified reactivity" of dye-loaded cavities are floating in vacuum.

### Collective vibronic cascade in cavity-coupled Jahn–Teller active molecules
_Anonymous (PRA)_ · [Phys. Rev. A](https://doi.org/10.1103/6wf6-kmtp) · PRA

Theory paper on cavity-coupled molecules with Jahn–Teller-active electronic structure, predicting a collective vibronic cascade through the polaritonic manifold. JT systems are interesting for VSC/electronic-SC discussions because the conical-intersection topology is itself sensitive to vibrational frequencies — so dressing the relevant vibrational modes via cavity coupling has well-defined consequences in principle. Worth reading critically alongside the Galego / Feist / Hammes-Schiffer literature on cavity-modified non-adiabatic dynamics; abstract-only access at the moment makes a deeper take premature.

### From single-particle to many-body chaos in the Yukawa–Sachdev–Ye–Kitaev model: Theory and a cavity-QED proposal
_Pascual Solis, Windey, Bandyopadhyay, Legramandi, Hauke_ · [Phys. Rev. B](https://doi.org/10.1103/wntd-53rd) · PRB

YSYK extends SYK by adding boson-mediated all-to-all fermionic interactions, and the authors map out how the model interpolates between SYK$_2$ (single-particle) and SYK$_4$ (full many-body) chaos as the interaction strength is tuned. The cQED-relevant punchline is a concrete proposal for an optical-cavity implementation with ultracold atoms, where the cavity photons mediate the Yukawa coupling. Stretches "cavity QED for chemistry" a bit, but the methodology — cavity-mediated bosonic glue between fermionic modes — is exactly the toolkit polaritonic-chemistry theorists keep reaching for, just deployed for many-body chaos instead of reaction rates.

## Trends & Themes

Two themes this week. First, **experiment-meets-No-Go**: the Scalari/Faist group's graphene result joins a longer arc of "the SRPT is harder to evade than the 2010s literature suggested," which is healthy. Second, **cavity QED as a quantum simulator** keeps creeping into condensed-matter theory papers — the YSYK proposal here is squarely in that lineage. On the chemistry side, the JACS ab initio collective-coupling work is the more consequential entry; once ab initio methods can reliably reproduce collective polariton spectra for real dyes, the field can finally start adjudicating the long-running VSC reaction-rate debates with calculations rather than vibes.
