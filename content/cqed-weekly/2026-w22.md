_A theory-heavy week: QEDFT pushes into intermolecular cavity-mediated coupling, an ab initio Green's-function machinery lands on exciton-polaritons, and a quantum-critical-meets-cavity proposal asks (again) whether photons can amplify what matter does on its own. Experiments are thinner — most of the loud "cavity" hits are sensors, atom arrays, or magnomechanics and were filtered out._

## Highlights

- **QEDFT goes intermolecular.** Aklilu et al. use real-time QED-TDDFT (Pauli–Fierz) to show one molecule can drive coherent dynamics in a distant, unexcited molecule through a shared cavity mode — a clean, first-principles look at the cavity-as-mediator story.
- **Polaritons from a Bethe–Salpeter–style Green's function.** Mauri et al. (Stanford / Yale) bring proper many-body machinery to exciton-polaritons and exciton localization, moving past model Hamiltonians for real materials.
- **Cavity coupling at a quantum critical point.** Sur, Si, and co. argue cavity-coupled quantum-critical matter shows an *amplified* response — interesting if real, but the regime is exactly where mean-field intuition fails hardest.

## Papers

### Quantum-electrodynamical time-dependent density functional theory description of cavity-mediated interactions between spatially separated molecules
_Aklilu, Yang, Covington, Varga_ · [Phys. Rev. Research, 10.1103/42x4-nlwy](https://doi.org/10.1103/42x4-nlwy) · QEDFT / ab initio cQED

Real-time QED-TDDFT on the Pauli–Fierz Hamiltonian: kick molecule A, watch molecule B (spatially separated) respond *only* when both are coupled to the same cavity mode. In free space, nothing; in the cavity, coherent transfer. This is the cleanest first-principles realization of the "cavity as a wire between molecules" picture that has been kicking around since Galego, Feist, and the Ruggenthaler/Rubio QEDFT line — and it's done without the usual single-mode model assumptions baked in by hand. Useful as a benchmark for the simpler model Hamiltonians people actually fit experiments with.

### Exciton-Polaritons and Exciton Localization from a First-Principles Interacting Green's Function Formalism
_Mauri, Ciccarino, Haber, Qiu, da Jornada_ · [Phys. Rev. Lett., 10.1103/j591-6f6f](https://doi.org/10.1103/j591-6f6f) · ab initio polaritons

A GW–BSE-style interacting Green's function treatment of exciton-polaritons that handles exciton localization on the same footing as the cavity coupling. The significance is methodological: most ab initio polariton work has been QED-DFT or QED-CC; pulling in the GW–BSE machinery means real materials with their actual self-energies and screening can be coupled to photons without reverting to two-level descriptions. Worth tracking as a candidate "production-grade" route for 2D-material polaritonics.

### Amplified response of cavity-coupled quantum-critical systems
_Sur, Wang, Mahankali, Paschen, Si_ · [Nat. Commun., 10.1038/s41467-026-73112-1](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-026-73112-1) · cavity-modified materials / quantum criticality

Theory claim: coupling a quantum-critical system to a cavity *amplifies* the response that's already singular at the critical point. The Si group has the right pedigree for the criticality side, and the question is timely given the ongoing debate about cavity-modified phase transitions (Faist, Kono, the SC-T_c controversies). The natural skeptical question — does the amplification survive once you treat the cavity nonperturbatively in the symmetry-broken phase, or is this a perturbative artifact? — is the one to watch in the followups.

### Meta-cavity quantum electrodynamics
_Authors not listed in metadata_ · [Phys. Rev. Lett., 10.1103/j8gx-58hf](https://doi.org/10.1103/j8gx-58hf) · cQED framework

PRL on "meta-cavity QED" — abstract not in the feed, so flagged for follow-up rather than summarized. ### Multimode-cavity picture of non-Markovian waveguide QED
_Cilluffo, Ferialdi, Palma, Calajò, Ciccarello_ · [Phys. Rev. Research, 10.1103/hc4r-2st8](https://doi.org/10.1103/hc4r-2st8) · waveguide / multimode cQED

Reframes non-Markovian waveguide QED — the regime of long retardation and delayed coherent feedback — as an effective lossy *multimode* cavity coupled to a white-noise bath. The payoff: the recently identified non-Markovian steady states can be recovered keeping just one or a few modes. A useful mental model for chemists: when "the cavity" is actually a structured photonic continuum, the multimode picture is the right intermediate language between full waveguide treatments and the single-mode Tavis–Cummings cartoon.

### Cavity-Reconstructed Exciton Relaxation and Charge Transfer in WS$_2$/MoS$_2$ Junctions
_Xu, Lu, Long_ · [J. Am. Chem. Soc., 10.1021/jacs.6c02510](https://doi.org/10.1021/jacs.6c02510) · cavity-modified TMD heterostructures

Computational study of how cavity coupling reshapes exciton relaxation and interlayer charge transfer in a WS$_2$/MoS$_2$ type-II junction. TMD heterobilayer charge transfer is one of the cleaner experimental platforms for testing cavity-modified photophysics, so a careful theory paper here is welcome. The thing to scrutinize is the coupling regime claimed and whether the cavity is treated as a real quantized mode (vs. a classical field tweak with a "polariton" relabel) — common failure mode in this subfield.

### Non-Hermitian modal analysis of plasmon-exciton coupling in open systems
_Li, Huang, Ge, Wang, Xu, Shao, Huang, Chen, Cao, Deng_ · [Phys. Rev. B, 10.1103/zx4x-5l88](https://doi.org/10.1103/zx4x-5l88) · plasmon-exciton polaritons

Non-Hermitian modal framework for plasmon-exciton coupling with realistic losses. Worth a look for anyone using lossy plasmonic nanocavities to claim strong coupling: it's a regular reminder that Rabi splitting in transmission spectra ≠ coherent quantum coupling once the dissipators are heavy.

### Inverse determination of light-matter coupling in disordered systems from transmittance spectra
_Authors not listed in metadata_ · [Phys. Rev. B, 10.1103/cggf-fpyb](https://doi.org/10.1103/cggf-fpyb) · disordered polaritonic spectroscopy

Inverse-problem approach: take a measured transmittance spectrum from a disordered ensemble and back out the underlying light-matter coupling distribution. If the inversion is robust, this is exactly the tool the VSC community needs to argue from spectra rather than assume a single $g$ in disordered molecular layers.

## Trends & Themes

Two complementary theory threads this week: (i) ab initio cQED is leaving the model-Hamiltonian sandbox — QEDFT now does intermolecular mediation, and GW–BSE-class methods are entering polariton-land; (ii) "what counts as the cavity" keeps getting renegotiated — multimode pictures for waveguide QED, meta-cavity framings in PRL, non-Hermitian modal bases for plasmonic strong coupling. Common subtext: single-mode Tavis–Cummings is increasingly the *starting point* people are leaving behind, not the punchline. Quiet week on the VSC-modifies-ground-state-chemistry front.
