_A quietly heavy week. The headline is an experimental claim of cavity-QED control over a correlated electronic phase (quantum Hall stripes) in Nature Physics, alongside a clean methods paper showing that QED-RPA = QED ring-CCD for ground-state polaritonic correlation. Theory continues to push collective-coupling phase transitions in solvable toy models._

## Highlights

- **Graziotto et al. (Nat. Phys.)** report cavity-induced suppression of longitudinal resistivity between quantum Hall plateaus — i.e. vacuum fields stabilizing thermally disordered stripes. If it holds up, this is one of the cleanest demonstrations that empty cavities can reshape a strongly correlated electronic phase.
- **DePrince, Yuwono & Eshuis (JCP)** extend the well-known RPA ↔ ring-CCD equivalence into the cavity-QED sector, giving QED-RPA a transparent diagrammatic foundation that includes photon creation channels. Useful infrastructure for the QED-DFT/QED-CC ecosystem.
- **Otake & Bamba (PRR)** show that a 1D Ising chain, which by Mermin–Wagner-adjacent reasoning has no finite-$T$ transition, acquires one once coupled to a cavity mode — an exactly solvable demonstration of photon-mediated superradiant ordering.

## Papers

### Cavity quantum electrodynamics control of quantum Hall stripes
_Graziotto, Enkner, Chattopadhyay, Curtis, Koskas et al._ · [Nat. Phys.](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41567-026-03287-3) · Nature Physics

A high-mobility 2DEG in a strong magnetic field is embedded in an engineered cavity; at ultralow $T$ and between Hall plateaus the longitudinal resistivity is suppressed below its $B=0$ value, interpreted as cavity-assisted stabilization of quantum Hall stripes (an anisotropic CDW phase of half-filled high Landau levels). This sits squarely in the "vacuum-field control of correlated matter" program alongside the Faist/Appugliese line, and is one of the more concrete experimental claims that engineered photon environments can pin a correlated electronic order. Worth watching for independent reproduction and for theoretical reconciliation with the (still-debated) collective-vs-single-particle scaling of these effects.

### Cavity quantum electrodynamics ring coupled cluster and the random phase approximation
_DePrince, Yuwono, Eshuis_ · [J. Chem. Phys.](https://doi.org/10.1063/5.0327154) · JCP

Generalizes the Scuseria–Henderson–Sorensen result (ph-RPA correlation energy equals ring-CCD) to cavity QED. The QED ring-CCD model retains double electronic excitations, mixed single-electron/single-photon excitations, and double-photon creation channels, and is shown to reproduce QED-RPA energies. Modest in scope but important plumbing: it puts QED-RPA on the same diagrammatic footing as its electronic ancestor and clarifies which photon processes the method does and does not capture — useful when comparing against QED-CCSD-12 or polaritonic-CC implementations.

### Exactly solvable phase transition in a cavity-coupled one-dimensional Ising chain
_Otake, Bamba_ · [Phys. Rev. Res.](https://doi.org/10.1103/fxml-nv46) · PRR

A 1D Ising chain coupled to a single cavity mode acquires a finite-temperature phase transition that the bare chain does not have. The photon mode mediates effective long-range fully-connected interactions on top of the local nearest-neighbour ones, and the model remains exactly solvable. It's a clean pedagogical example of how cavity-mediated infinite-range couplings can rescue ordering in low-dimensional systems, and complements the recent wave of no-go / yes-go debates around extensive-vs-intensive collective coupling.

### Photoinduced superconducting diode effect via chiral cavity modes
_Arora, Narang_ · [Phys. Rev. Res.](https://doi.org/10.1103/t19v-dmwd) · PRR

Proposes that photon exchange with chiral cavity modes can break time-reversal symmetry in a superconductor's many-body ground state via photon-induced orbital magnetization, yielding a nonreciprocal critical-current (diode) response. Worked out for twisted bilayer graphene but pitched as generic. Theoretical; the interesting move is dressing the SC ground state with chiral vacuum fluctuations rather than driving it with a circularly polarized pump. Plausible mechanism, but the magnitude of induced orbital magnetization from realistic cavity fields will decide whether this is observable or curiosity.

### Quench Instabilities of a Strongly Interacting Quantum Gas in an Optical Cavity
_Marijanović, Chattopadhyay, Skolc, Zwettler, Halati et al._ · [Phys. Rev. Lett.](https://doi.org/10.1103/w2vx-t1mr) · PRL

Studies dynamical instabilities after a quench in a strongly interacting atomic gas coupled to an optical cavity — the cavity-induced long-range interactions can drive runaway pattern-forming instabilities on top of the contact-interaction background. Continues the Zürich/Innsbruck tradition of using cold-atom-in-cavity setups as analog simulators of self-organized phases, now in the strongly interacting regime where mean-field treatments are not safe.

### Cavity-controlled topological phase transitions in dipolar arrays via asymmetric design
_Wu, Liu, Xiao_ · [Phys. Rev. B](https://doi.org/10.1103/y51b-7m9b) · PRB

Photon-mediated couplings in asymmetric dipolar lattices are used to drive topological band-structure transitions, controlled by cavity geometry. Sits in the broader programme of using cavity-photon-mediated long-range hopping as a knob for topology. Theory-only; the asymmetric-design angle is the novelty over earlier symmetric photonic-crystal proposals.

### A Scalable Platform for High Cooperativity and Multimode Ultrastrong Plasmon–Phonon Coupling
_Yu, Ma, Tan, Yao, Cao_ · [ACS Photonics](https://doi.org/10.1021/acsphotonics.6c00257) · ACS Photonics

Experimental platform reaching ultrastrong plasmon–phonon coupling with multiple phonon modes simultaneously and high cooperativity. Plasmon–phonon polaritons are the IR/THz cousins of vibro-polaritons and matter for the VSC-modified-chemistry debate by analogy. Worth a look for groups thinking about how to bring USC into the mid-IR for vibrational-strong-coupling chemistry without microfluidic Fabry–Pérot stacks.

### Strong Coupling to Higher-Order Photon Mode Enhances Emission Yield in Organic Microcavities
_Cho, Schwennicke, Cahill, Lakhwani_ · [ACS Photonics](https://doi.org/10.1021/acsphotonics.5c03055) · ACS Photonics

Reports that coupling organic emitters to higher-order (rather than the usual fundamental) photon modes of a microcavity enhances emission yield. The mechanism plausibly involves better overlap with the upper-polariton radiative pathway, but the polariton-relaxation literature is famously messy — worth reading the SI carefully for how dark-state population and triplet leakage are handled before taking the enhancement at face value.

### Observation of an aperiodic polariton monotile
_Alyatkin, Kartashov, Sitnik, Grigoryev, Lagoudakis_ · [arXiv:2605.13206](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13206) · cond-mat.quant-gas

Exciton-polariton condensates are optically sculpted on the vertices of "einstein" aperiodic monotiles in an inorganic microcavity, and the resulting phase-locked condensate shows six-fold Bragg peaks and Dirac-like spectral features distinct from both periodic triangular and Penrose tilings. Lovely use of the polariton platform as a programmable driven-dissipative simulator; whether the synchronization patterns survive in the thermodynamic limit / under disorder is the obvious next question.

## Trends & Themes

The week's centre of gravity is **cavity-mediated phase control of correlated matter**: an experimental hit (Hall stripes), a solvable toy model (cavity-coupled Ising), and two theory proposals (SC diode, topological dipolar arrays). On the chemistry side it was a quieter week — one clean methods paper (QED-RPA ↔ QED ring-CCD) and no major VSC-modified-chemistry claims. The polariton-condensate community continues to deliver pretty geometry experiments.
