_A heavier-than-usual cQED week (7 arXiv hits). The polaritonic-chemistry reproducibility debate continues with a fresh call for reliable spectroscopic frameworks. On the physics side, a clean experimental demonstration of UV exciton-polaritons in a new layered material, the first direct probe of dynamical universality in a driven quantum fluid of light, and a self-consistent mQED treatment of dispersion interactions. Plus a curious application of the Pontus-Mpemba effect to cavity QED._

## Highlights

- **Polaritonic chemistry's reliability crisis goes from grumble to framework proposal** — a paper devoted explicitly to building the spectroscopic kinetics framework the field has been missing.
- **UV polaritonics gets a new material** — silver phenylthiolate (Thiorene) supports UV exciton-polaritons in a natural multi-quantum-well vdW architecture, opening short-wavelength polariton chemistry/photonics.
- **Dynamical critical scaling, observed directly** — driven exciton-polariton condensate below threshold, probed in the fluctuation-dominated disordered phase, gives one of the first direct experimental access points to non-equilibrium critical exponents.

## Papers

### Toward Reliable Spectroscopic Analysis of Reaction Kinetics in Polaritonic Chemistry
_authors et al._ · [arXiv:2605.06002](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06002) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-05-07
Names the problem directly: VSC reaction-rate-modification experiments have repeatedly failed to reproduce, and the proposed vibrational-polariton mechanism remains contested. Builds toward a reliable theoretical framework for kinetics-spectroscopy analysis under VSC. The kind of "let's slow down and define what we're measuring" paper the field has needed since 2019.

### Ultraviolet Exciton-Polaritons in Silver Phenylthiolate
_authors et al._ · [arXiv:2605.03748](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03748) · physics.optics · 2026-05-04
Demonstrates UV polariton formation in **Thiorene (AgSPh)**, a vdW layered metal-organic chalcogenolate that naturally forms multi-quantum-well architecture with a sharp 3.46 eV in-plane exciton. UV polaritonics has been bottlenecked by material availability — large oscillator strength + narrow + UV is rare. This expands the platform.

### Dynamical universality in a driven quantum fluid of light
_authors et al._ · [arXiv:2605.02397](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02397) · cond-mat.quant-gas · 2026-05-04
**Direct experimental measurement of dynamical critical scaling** in a driven-dissipative exciton-polariton condensate below threshold. Probes the fluctuation-dominated disordered phase to extract growth of spatial correlations + slowing dynamics. Bridges polariton experiments to the broader nonequilibrium critical-phenomena program.

### Self-consistent radiative backaction in dispersion interactions: a minimal mQED model
_authors et al._ · [arXiv:2605.02981](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02981) · quant-ph · 2026-05-04
Macroscopic QED treatment of van der Waals interactions when **transition dipole moments and excitation energies are allowed to respond to the interaction itself**. Three-level model shows one-sided self-energy effects are short-ranged but full mutual backaction has longer reach. Conceptually useful for any dispersion-force computation where the interacting systems aren't electronically rigid.

### Pontus-Mpemba effect in cavity quantum electrodynamics
_authors et al._ · [arXiv:2605.05827](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05827) · quant-ph · 2026-05-07
Proposes a realization of the quantum Mpemba-type effect (faster relaxation through a two-step protocol than an unquenched one) in the Jaynes-Cummings model with photon loss. Single-excitation sector analysis; minimal-but-real setup. A specific dissipative-dynamics phenomenon whose experimental realization in cavity QED would make the broader Mpemba program concrete.

## Trends & Themes

The cQED chemistry side is in **clean-up mode** this week — building reliable analysis frameworks rather than chasing new effects. The cQED physics side is **delivering**: a new material for UV polaritons, direct measurement of nonequilibrium criticality, and increasingly sophisticated mQED treatments of light-matter response. The two sides may be diverging in tempo.
