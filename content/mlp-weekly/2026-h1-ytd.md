_The first four months of 2026 made one thing clear: universal MLIPs are no longer a research curiosity. The conversation has shifted from "can a single model cover the periodic table?" to "where do these models still fail, and how do we patch them?" This YTD digest gathers ~50 representative arXiv preprints from January through late April 2026, grouped by theme — the architectural scaling story, the long-range/electrostatics correction wave, the maturing transition-state and active-learning toolchains, and a steady drumbeat of materials, battery, and water applications._

_Each entry is one or two sentences. Read it as a map of the territory, not a literature review._

---

## 1. Universal & foundation MLIPs

The dominant theme of 2026 so far: bigger models, broader coverage, and honest reckoning with their limits.

### Breaking the Training Barrier of Billion-Parameter Universal Machine Learning Interatomic Potentials
[arXiv:2604.15821](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15821) · cs.DC · 2026-04-17
A parallel training framework that finally makes billion-parameter uMLIPs trainable, addressing the second-derivative bottleneck that had blocked scaling. Of interest mainly for groups with the compute to push past ~100M parameters.

### A Lightweight Universal Machine-Learning Interatomic Potential via Knowledge Distillation
[arXiv:2604.10887](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.10887) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci · 2026-04-13
SevenNet-Nano: distill a large multi-task foundation model (SevenNet-Omni) into a much smaller student that keeps most of its generalization. The pragmatic counterweight to the billion-parameter trend.

### Suiren-1.0 Technical Report: A Family of Molecular Foundation Models
[arXiv:2603.21942](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.21942) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-03-23
A 1.8B-parameter molecular foundation model pretrained on a 70M-sample DFT corpus, with sibling variants for dimers and conformer averaging. The molecular-side analogue of the materials uMLIP race.

### Matlantis-PFP v8: Universal MLIP with Better Experimental Agreements via r²SCAN
[arXiv:2603.11063](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.11063) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-03-09
PFP retrained on r²SCAN rather than PBE, with the explicit goal of better zero-shot agreement against experiment rather than DFT self-consistency. A useful reframing — what we should be benchmarking against has been an open question.

### Scaling Machine Learning Interatomic Potentials with Mixtures of Experts
[arXiv:2603.07977](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.07977) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-03-09
Sparse MoE applied to MLIPs: shared experts plus sparse activation deliver real expressivity gains, and nonlinear MoE beats the linear variant. A clean transplant of an LLM-era trick.

### Expanding Universal MLIPs to 97 Elements Toward Nuclear Applications
[arXiv:2603.03223](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03223) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-03-03
Extends element coverage to include minor actinides — a niche but real gap, since most current uMLIPs leave the heavy end of the table dark. Targeted at fuel-cycle and irradiation modeling.

### MatRIS: Toward Reliable and Efficient Pretrained MLIPs
[arXiv:2603.02002](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.02002) · cs.LG · 2026-03-02
Argues that the tensor-product machinery of equivariant MLIPs is overkill at foundation scale, and shows competitive accuracy with a cheaper alternative. Part of a wider 2026 push to question whether full equivariance is worth its price tag.

### MACE-POLAR-1: A Polarisable Electrostatic Foundation Model for Molecular Chemistry
[arXiv:2602.19411](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.19411) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-02-23
Extends MACE with explicit long-range electrostatics and induction. The first foundation-scale MLIP that takes polarization seriously rather than absorbing it into local features.

### UBio-MolFM: A Universal Molecular Foundation Model for Bio-Systems
[arXiv:2602.17709](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.17709) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-02-13
A bio-specific foundation model with a multi-fidelity dataset (UBio-Mol26) designed to bridge QM accuracy and biological scale. Closer to the protein/MD audience than typical uMLIPs.

### Pushing the Limits of Unconstrained Machine-Learned Interatomic Potentials
[arXiv:2601.16195](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.16195) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-01-22
A provocative finding: relaxing strict equivariance and energy-conservation constraints can improve accuracy and efficiency, not just speed. The empirical case for "constraint-light" architectures keeps growing.

## 2. Architectures, scaling, and expressivity

Less hype, more engineering: the 2026 architecture papers are mostly about making equivariant nets faster without giving back accuracy.

### EquiformerV3: Scaling Efficient, Expressive, and General SE(3)-Equivariant Graph Attention Transformers
[arXiv:2604.09130](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.09130) · cs.LG · 2026-04-10
Iteration on EquiformerV2 with efficiency, expressivity, and generality improvements across the board. Now the de facto reference architecture for SE(3)-equivariant attention.

### Hessian-Informed Machine Learning Interatomic Potential
[arXiv:2603.25373](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25373) · cs.LG · 2026-03-26
Train MLIPs to match Hessians, not just energies and forces, so curvature-dependent observables (vibrations, free energies, transition states) actually behave. A simple fix that should have happened sooner.

### A Recipe for Scalable Attention-Based MLIPs (AllScAIP)
[arXiv:2603.06567](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06567) · cs.LG · 2026-03-06
Pure attention without explicit physics terms can recover long-range accuracy via all-to-all node attention. Tests the question of whether physics priors are still load-bearing at scale.

### MARA: Continuous SE(3)-Equivariant Attention for Molecular Force Fields
[arXiv:2602.02671](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.02671) · cs.LG · 2026-02-02
Replaces fixed angular expansions with learnable continuous spherical attention. A cleaner abstraction than discrete L-channels.

### From Evaluation to Design: PES Smoothness Metrics to Guide MLIP Architectures
[arXiv:2602.04861](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.04861) · cs.LG · 2026-02-04
Argues that energy/force regression error misses important failure modes; introduces smoothness metrics that catch them. Should become standard ablation practice.

### E2Former-V2: On-the-Fly Equivariant Attention with Linear Activation Memory
[arXiv:2601.16622](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.16622) · cs.LG · 2026-01-23
Axis-aligned sparsification gets equivariant attention to linear memory. Practical for biomolecular-scale systems where dense edge tensor products are infeasible.

### Equivariant Interatomic Potentials without Tensor Products
[arXiv:2601.15492](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.15492) · physics.comp-ph · 2026-01-21
Drops Clebsch-Gordan tensor products entirely while preserving equivariance, removing the cost-vs-resolution tradeoff at the heart of MACE-style models.

## 3. Long-range interactions & electrostatics

The locality assumption finally cracked. Multiple groups converged on similar fixes in Q1.

### MACE-POLAR-1
See §1 above — the highest-profile entry, and a foundation model rather than a one-off.

### qNEP: Neuroevolution Potential with Dynamic Charges
[arXiv:2601.19034](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.19034) · physics.comp-ph · 2026-01-26
Adds environment-dependent partial charges to NEP at low overhead, enabling dielectric response and IR spectra at large scale. Also reused in the Na-battery SEI study below.

### An SO(3)-Equivariant Reciprocal-Space Neural Potential for Long-Range Interactions
[arXiv:2603.18389](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.18389) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-03-19
Long-range electrostatics via a reciprocal-space head that preserves SO(3) equivariance and energy-force consistency, addressing the standard tradeoffs of prior LR extensions.

### Long-Range MLIPs with Environment-Dependent Charges Enable Predicting LO-TO Splitting and Dielectric Constants
[arXiv:2603.06396](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06396) · physics.comp-ph · 2026-03-06
Augments Moment Tensor Potentials with point charges (with optional total-charge conservation), recovering optical phonon splittings and dielectric responses from simple ML models.

### Design Space of Self-Consistent Electrostatic MLIPs
[arXiv:2603.14700](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.14700) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-03-16
A clean taxonomy of electrostatic-MLIP designs and where each one breaks. Worth reading before picking an LR scheme off the shelf.

### Physics-Informed Long-Range Coulomb Correction for ML Hamiltonians
[arXiv:2603.20007](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20007) · physics.comp-ph · 2026-03-20
Closed-form Coulomb correction for ML Hamiltonians via variational decomposition, plugging the long-range gap that standard models leave open in polar crystals.

### False Metallization in Short-Ranged Machine Learned Interatomic Potentials
[arXiv:2603.04228](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04228) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-03-04
Shows that locality-only MLIPs produce unphysical dipolar alignment at polar interfaces — a concrete failure mode that explains some long-standing weirdness in interface MD.

### Scalable Machine Learning Force Fields for Macromolecular Systems Through Long-Range Aware Message Passing
[arXiv:2601.03774](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.03774) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-01-07
Force prediction error in fixed-cutoff MLFFs scales monotonically with system size — quantifies the locality penalty for proteins explicitly.

### Explicit Electric Potential-Embedded Machine Learning Framework
[arXiv:2604.07322](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07322) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-04-08
Predicts atomic forces and electron density jointly under an explicit applied potential, aimed at constant-potential ab initio MD for electrochemistry.

## 4. Reactions, transition states, and catalysis

Transition-state finding got several mutually-complementary tools this quarter.

### Reliable and Efficient Automated Transition-State Searches with Machine-Learned Interatomic Potentials
[arXiv:2604.00405](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.00405) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-04-01
Benchmarks six freely-available potentials (MACE-OMol25, UMA-Small/Medium, others) on hybrid TS-search workflows. The first systematic look at how reliable these are out of the box.

### Fine-Tuning Universal MLPs for Transition State Search in Surface Catalysis
[arXiv:2603.24482](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.24482) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci · 2026-03-25
Active-learning-driven fine-tuning workflow that gets uMLPs to the accuracy needed for reactive surface configurations. Bridges the gap between transferable foundation models and task-specific accuracy.

### Machine-Learned Leftmost Hessian Eigenvectors for Robust Transition State Finding
[arXiv:2603.21323](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.21323) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-03-22
Predicts only the most negative Hessian eigenvector — the reaction coordinate locally — so TS optimizers get curvature info without paying for full Hessians.

### A Priori Sampling of Transition States with Guided Diffusion
[arXiv:2603.25980](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25980) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-03-26
Generates TS candidates via guided diffusion without heuristic reaction-coordinate assumptions. Promising for ensembles of competing pathways.

### PFP/MM: Hybrid uMLIP + Classical Force Field for Large-Scale Reactive Simulations
[arXiv:2603.16061](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.16061) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci · 2026-03-17
Couples PFP (universal NN potential) to MM for the bulk, getting reactive QM/MM-like simulations at scale without paying uMLIP cost everywhere. Sensible engineering.

### MAD-SURF: MLIP for Molecular Adsorption on Coinage Metal Surfaces
[arXiv:2601.18852](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.18852) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci · 2026-01-26
Surface-chemistry-specialized MLIP for organic adsorbates on Cu/Ag/Au, with the SPM-interpretation use case in mind.

### MLIP-MC: Adsorption Simulations Using MLIPs
[arXiv:2602.13725](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.13725) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci · 2026-02-14
Open-source GCMC framework that runs zero-shot uMLIP-driven adsorption screening for MOFs. The kind of glue infrastructure that lowers the barrier to using uMLIPs in practice.

### Data-Driven MLIP for Gas-Surface Scattering: NO on Graphite
[arXiv:2603.18864](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.18864) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-03-19
Workflow targeted specifically at scattering dynamics, where standard MLIP datasets undersample high-energy collisions. A useful reminder that "good for MD" doesn't mean "good for everything".

## 5. Data, active learning, and uncertainty

Less new architecture, more attention to what we feed these models.

### Errors That Matter: Uncertainty-Aware Universal MLPs Calibrated on Experiments
[arXiv:2604.24607](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24607) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-04-27
Calibrates uMLP uncertainty against experiments, not just DFT, exposing electronic-structure-method bias that internal UQ misses entirely.

### NEPMaker: Active Learning for NEP at Large Cells
[arXiv:2604.13848](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13848) · physics.comp-ph · 2026-04-15
D-optimality-based active learning inside GPUMD's NEP, targeted at large-cell simulations where labeling whole configurations is impractical.

### Data Curation for MLIPs by Determinantal Point Processes
[arXiv:2603.22160](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.22160) · stat.AP · 2026-03-23
DPPs to pick informative subsets of configurations to label. A nicely-motivated alternative to ad-hoc diversity heuristics.

### High-Quality, High-Information Datasets for Universal Atomistic Machine Learning
[arXiv:2603.02089](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.02089) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci · 2026-03-02
Argues most existing electronic-structure datasets are screening-oriented rather than force-field-oriented, and proposes principles for building MLIP-grade ones. Likely to influence the next generation of training corpora.

### On-the-Fly Outlier Detection for Robust MLIP Training
[arXiv:2602.08849](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.08849) · stat.ML · 2026-02-09
Detects and downweights numerical-noise outliers in training data without retraining cycles, addressing a problem most groups currently solve by hand.

### Bias in Universal MLIPs and Its Effects on Fine-Tuning
[arXiv:2603.10159](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.10159) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci · 2026-03-10
Quantifies systematic biases in current uMLIPs and shows fine-tuning is required for OOD accuracy. Important counterweight to "just use the foundation model" optimism.

### Enhanced Representation-Based Sampling for MLIP Datasets (ERBS)
[arXiv:2601.16047](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.16047) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-01-22
Auto-discovers collective variables from atomic descriptors and biases sampling along them — gets diverse training sets without hand-designed CVs.

### Active Learning Strategies for Efficient MLIPs Across Diverse Material Systems
[arXiv:2601.06916](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.06916) · cs.LG · 2026-01-11
Compares four AL strategies (random, uncertainty, diversity, hybrid) on Materials Project + OQMD. Practical guidance, not flashy.

### AutoPot: Automated and Massively Parallelized Construction of MLIPs
[arXiv:2601.01185](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.01185) · physics.comp-ph · 2026-01-03
End-to-end automated MLIP construction with parallel data generation. Targets the "single training set never works for all applications" problem head-on.

## 6. Materials, alloys, and phase behavior

Where the rubber meets the road. Most of these are application papers, but several push potential design alongside the science.

### Million-Atom Simulations of Multicomponent Alloys
[arXiv:2604.01642](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01642) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci · 2026-04-02
Compares NEP and graph ACE (GRACE) across 16 elemental metals and multicomponent alloys at million-atom scale. GRACE-Finnis-Sinclair wins on training efficiency.

### General-Purpose Machine-Learned Potential for CrCoNi Alloys
[arXiv:2603.25616](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25616) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci · 2026-03-26
Composition-transferable potential for CrCoNi capturing short-range order and stacking-fault energy across the off-equimolar phase space. The kind of dedicated potential uMLIPs still don't fully replace.

### Aluminum Solidification and Nanopolycrystal Deformation via GNN Potential
[arXiv:2603.24360](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.24360) · physics.comp-ph · 2026-03-25
Million-atom solidification + plasticity for Al with a graph-NN potential — direct atomistic look at nucleation and defects in metal AM.

### Accurate and Efficient Interatomic Potentials for Dislocations in InP
[arXiv:2604.20515](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20515) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci · 2026-04-22
ACE and MACE models on a new RSCAN dataset, validated against the InP dislocation literature. Sets a higher bar for compound-semiconductor MLIPs.

### Machine Learning Determines the Mg2SiO4 P-T Phase Diagram
[arXiv:2602.01730](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.01730) · physics.geo-ph · 2026-02-02
Maps wadsleyite/ringwoodite phase boundaries via MLIP-driven non-equilibrium thermodynamic integration — the kind of free-energy work that's becoming routine with these tools.

### Atomic-Scale Order Enables High Thermal Boundary Conductance at β-Ga₂O₃/4H-SiC Interfaces
[arXiv:2604.15394](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15394) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci · 2026-04-16
MLIP + lattice dynamics framework that links interfacial disorder to TBC quantitatively. Useful template for other dissimilar-material interface studies.

### Neuroevolution Potential for Gallium Oxide
[arXiv:2601.10174](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.10174) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci · 2026-01-15
NEP for Ga₂O₃ with energy-dependent treatment, capturing all polymorphs and swift-heavy-ion irradiation. A model for handling polymorph-rich oxides.

### Machine-Learned Potential for Amorphous Indium-Tin-Oxide Alloys
[arXiv:2601.00145](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.00145) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci · 2026-01-01
Bespoke MLIP for amorphous ITO MD, targeting display and reconfigurable color-display device modeling.

### Computational Design of Ductile AM Tungsten-Based Refractory Alloys
[arXiv:2601.11295](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.11295) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci · 2026-01-16
MLIP-accelerated alloy design to overcome tungsten's printability problem for fusion-reactor components.

### Interatomic Potentials for Platinum
[arXiv:2602.00379](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.00379) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci · 2026-01-30
ADP and modified-Tersoff Pt potentials beating prior literature potentials against DFT and experiment. A reminder that classical functional forms still get developed alongside the GNN tide.

### Neutron and X-ray Diffraction Reveal the Limits of Long-Range MLIPs for Medium-Range Order in Silica Glass
[arXiv:2604.21222](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21222) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci · 2026-04-23
Locality-only MLIPs reproduce SiO₄ tetrahedra but miss the first sharp diffraction peak of silica glass. Concrete experimental anchor for the long-range debate above.

## 7. Batteries, electrolytes, and interfaces

A focused subdomain that punched above its weight in Q1 — interfaces are where the locality assumption hurts most.

### Differentiable Hybrid Force Fields for Autonomous Electrolyte Discovery
[arXiv:2604.07979](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07979) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci · 2026-04-09
Calibratable hybrid FF that satisfies the speed/accuracy/calibratability trilemma for electrolyte high-throughput screening. Differentiability is the new table stake.

### Mechanisms of SEI Formation and Sodium Loss via Interface Reactor Sampling
[arXiv:2604.07077](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07077) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci · 2026-04-08
qNEP (charge-aware NEP) used in an "Interface Reactor" sampling strategy to resolve SEI formation in Na-metal batteries. A nice case where charge-awareness directly enabled the science.

### AQVolt26: High-Temperature r²SCAN Halide Dataset
[arXiv:2604.02524](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.02524) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci · 2026-04-02
A halide solid-electrolyte dataset specifically built to stress-test general-purpose uMLIPs on dynamically soft systems. Worth tracking as a benchmark.

### Electrolyte Solvation Structure from an OMol25-Trained Potential
[arXiv:2603.20183](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20183) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-03-20
First MLIP results from the OMol25 dataset, with experimental validation of solvation structure. Likely an early entry in what will be a long line of OMol25-derived models.

### Overcoming Sampling Limitations in Water-in-Salt Electrolytes Using MLIPs
[arXiv:2603.22099](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.22099) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-03-23
MACE potentials evaluated on a 21 m water-in-salt LiTFSI electrolyte across out-of-the-box, fine-tuned, and from-scratch regimes. Honest accounting of where each option pays off.

### Are Universal Potentials Ready for Alkali-Ion Battery Kinetics?
[arXiv:2601.10938](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.10938) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci · 2026-01-16
Benchmarks M3GNet, CHGNet, MACE, SevenNet, GRACE, Orb against DFT for cathode and SE migration barriers. Orb-v3 wins on statics; details get nuanced for dynamics.

### Benchmarking Short-Range MLIPs for Metal/Electrolyte Interfaces
[arXiv:2602.22931](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.22931) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-02-26
Calls out failure modes specific to metal/electrolyte interfaces under standard periodic DFT references — a practical companion to the false-metallization paper.

### AI-Ready Fine-Tuning Framework for Solid-Solid Battery Interfaces (FIRE)
[arXiv:2601.17847](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.17847) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci · 2026-01-25
Replay-augmented continual fine-tuning of uMLIPs for the heterogeneity of solid-solid interfaces. A cleaner pattern than re-training from scratch per interface.

## 8. Water and quantum nuclear effects

A small, sharp cluster of papers — water continues to be the proving ground for high-accuracy MLIP work.

### Understanding the Density Maximum of Water with Machine-Learned Potentials
[arXiv:2603.27767](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.27767) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-03-29
Reproduces the 4 °C density maximum from first principles and decomposes the structural origin. The kind of result that's only just become routine with MLIPs.

### Accurate Thermophysical Properties of Water Using Machine-Learned Potentials
[arXiv:2601.21103](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.21103) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-01-28
MACE-trained water model with quantitative thermophysical accuracy and tractable UQ. Closer to engineering-grade water simulation.

### More Converged, Less Accurate? Reassessing Standard Choices for Ab Initio Water
[arXiv:2603.20451](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20451) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-03-20
Shows that converging electronic-structure settings can make MLIP-driven water properties *worse* — a useful and unsettling result for anyone who picks DFT settings by habit.

### Ab Initio Simulation of the First-Order Proton-Ordering Transition in Water Ice
[arXiv:2603.09247](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09247) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci · 2026-03-10
Captures the meV-scale ice Ih → ice XI ordering transition, a regime where most potentials lack the resolution to discriminate hydrogen-bond configurations.

### Quantum-Corrected NMR Crystallography at Scale
[arXiv:2603.06236](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06236) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-03-06
Combines high-level shielding predictions with nuclear quantum corrections so chemical-shift-driven NMR crystallography stops failing on H-bonded protons.

### Infrared Spectroscopy of Protonated Water Clusters via Quantum Thermal Bath + ML Potentials
[arXiv:2603.09410](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09410) · physics.comp-ph · 2026-01-10
Quantum thermal bath driving an MLIP gives quantitative IR spectra of small protonated clusters. Path-integral-style accuracy at force-field cost.

### Refinement and Performance Benchmark for Range-Separated Water Force Field
[arXiv:2601.18416](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.18416) · physics.chem-ph · 2026-01-26
Iteration on a CCSD(T)-anchored water FF, addressing training instability from sparse high-level reference data.

## Trends and themes

A few patterns from the quarter that cut across themes:

- **The locality assumption is over.** At least four independent groups (MACE-POLAR-1, qNEP, the SO(3) reciprocal-space potential, AllScAIP, the Coulomb-corrected ML Hamiltonian) shipped long-range solutions in Q1. Expect locality-only foundation models to be a minority by year-end.
- **"Constraint-light" architectures are credible.** Multiple papers (MatRIS, "Pushing the limits of unconstrained MLIPs", Equivariant Potentials without Tensor Products) argue that strict equivariance is overpriced at scale. The verdict isn't in, but the prior has shifted.
- **Distillation is the answer to the bigness problem.** SevenNet-Nano makes the case that we don't need everyone to run billion-parameter models; we need to be able to compress them. This will become standard.
- **Benchmarks are getting harder and more honest.** Errors-That-Matter calibrates against experiment, not DFT. AQVolt26 specifically targets a dynamically soft system. Bias-in-uMLIPs quantifies the OOD penalty. This is healthy.
- **TS finding is becoming a solved-enough problem.** Four complementary papers cover the space (benchmark, AL fine-tuning, eigenvector prediction, guided diffusion). Expect TS searches to be a routine downstream uMLIP task by mid-2026.
