_A heavy week for MLIP infrastructure: a major mlip v2 release with an eSEN Mixture-of-Experts backbone, a multi-fidelity scheme that pushes MACE toward QMC accuracy, a sharper entropy-driven active learning recipe, and a category-theoretic alternative to equivariant networks that recovers the Wigner–Eckart rules from QM9 with no quantum input. Applications span Li-ion cathodes, halide double perovskites, polymer-derived ceramics, and defective UN thermal transport._

## Highlights

- **mlip v2 + eSEN MoE** ([Brunken et al.](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22698)) — Major library rewrite with a Mixture-of-Experts equivariant backbone (eSEN), a high-performance e3j kernel, NPT + NEB, and more physical long-range/electrostatics. The first MoE MLIP framework that looks production-ready.
- **QMC-fine-tuned MACE** ([Hložný et al.](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22601)) — Multi-fidelity training: high-level QMC energies + DFT forces, fine-tune only MACE readouts. Near-QMC barriers for S-vacancy migration in MoS₂ from a *fairly limited* QMC dataset. Real path to QMC-quality MD.
- **$\star_G$ tensor algebra** ([Hoyos et al.](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.20440)) — Algebraic, not architectural, equivariance: a $\star_G$-SVD with Eckart–Young optimality, Lean-verified. On QM9 it recovers the Wigner–Eckart selection rules from data alone at ~50–90× fewer parameters than matched MLPs. Provocative complement (not replacement) to ENNs.

## Papers

### mlip v2: Advancing Open-Source Software for Efficient and Scalable Molecular Simulation
_Brunken, Cormier, Walewski, et al._ · [arXiv:2605.22698](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22698) · physics.chem-ph

A v2 rewrite of the mlip library with a redesigned API, a new equivariant kernel (`e3j`), and — most notably — the eSEN architecture with a Mixture-of-Experts formulation aimed at scaling training across heterogeneous datasets. New physics features: more grounded charge modeling, long-range interaction handling, NPT and NEB. MoE for MLIPs has been talked about for a while; this is the first mainstream framework shipping it with the surrounding tooling to actually use it.

### Upscaling DFT-trained MLIPs toward QMC accuracy via S-vacancy migration in MoS₂
_Hložný, Brndiar, Luo, Štich_ · [arXiv:2605.22601](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22601) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Multi-fidelity recipe: train a MACE baseline on DFT energies+forces, then fine-tune only the readout layers using high-level QMC energies (sidestepping the well-known difficulty of QMC forces). Demonstrated on mono- and multi-S-vacancy migration in monolayer MoS₂, with strong in- and out-of-domain accuracy gains. The headline is the data budget: surprisingly few QMC points materially shift the model. If this transfers beyond TMDs it's a real argument for QMC-fine-tuning as a default top-up step.

### Benchmarking MPNN potentials for molecular IR spectroscopy
_Bhatia, Krejci, Rinke_ · [arXiv:2605.22367](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22367) · physics.chem-ph

Head-to-head of SchNet, FieldSchNet, SO3Net, PaiNN, and MACE on energies, forces, dipoles, harmonic frequencies, and full MD-derived IR spectra. Conclusions are roughly the ones the community has been circling: invariant models (SchNet/FieldSchNet) underperform on transfer; equivariant models generalize; PaiNN is the best efficiency/accuracy trade-off; MACE wins on spectral fidelity. Useful as a reference table when picking a model for vibrational work.

### TriForces: Augmenting Atomistic GNNs for Transferable Representations
_Ramlaoui, Duval, Bull, et al._ · [arXiv:2605.20581](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.20581) · cs.LG

Three-stream, model-agnostic framework that separates composition and structure information and uses self-supervised objectives to keep both streams accessible in the latent space. Without any DFT labels it improves MatBench/QM9 baselines; on OMat24 at 20K samples it cuts energy MAE by 57%. Pretrained variants are released. A clean answer to the "MLIPs lose composition info" complaint.

### Group-algebraic tensors: provably optimal equivariant learning + symmetry discovery
_Hoyos, Ubaru, Huh, et al._ · [arXiv:2605.20440](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.20440) · cs.LG

Defines a $\star_G$ algebra where any finite group $G$ sets the multiplication rule, making equivariance algebraic rather than architectural. Comes with a $\star_G$-SVD that has an Eckart–Young optimality proof (formalized in 600 lines of Lean 4), a Kronecker rule to compose symmetries, and — most striking — a fit-the-group-from-data capability. On QM9, decomposing geometries over the chiral octahedral subgroup of SO(3) **rediscovers Wigner–Eckart selection rules** with zero QM input. Closed-form ridge predictors at ~50–90× fewer parameters than matched MLPs. This is not going to replace equivariant GNNs, but it offers interpretability and compression they structurally can't.

### Dataset-aware entropy-maximized active learning for MLIPs
_Wang, Rao, Zhu_ · [arXiv:2605.20384](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.20384) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Combines per-configuration entropy (to diversify MD snapshots) with a global log-det covariance criterion (to admit only genuinely informative additions), using analytical fingerprint gradients from Gaussian overlap eigenvalues and an Allegro-OAM-L foundation model. On C, Si, and NaCl, the entropy strategy lowers energy MAE 3–10× vs random-MD sampling at 800 structures. The clean recipe (per-atom vs per-config covariance switch by phase character) and the foundation-model warm start are the practical bits to copy.

### Direct simulation of LiNi₀.₈Mn₀.₁Co₀.₁O₂ transport with an MLP
_He, van de Wetering, Nolsen, Artrith_ · [arXiv:2605.19747](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19747) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Fine-tunes a MACE foundation model on a near-ground-state NMC811 dataset, uses active learning, then runs large-scale MD to get Li self-diffusion coefficients directly — keeping DFT-quality energetics while breaking past DFT time/length scales. A nice template for cathode workflows: foundation MLP → small AL loop → direct transport observables rather than NEB barriers.

### Generative Pseudo-Force Fields for molecular generation
_Hessmann, Kahouli, Gugler, Plainer, Noé, Müller, Gebauer_ · [arXiv:2605.19050](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19050) · cs.LG

Bridges MLFFs and diffusion models by training an MLFF on a quadratic *pseudo-potential* around equilibria — no ab-initio for the perturbed geometries; noise is added on the fly. Shows formally that GPFF is a time-step-agnostic variant of variance-exploding diffusion, so the force magnitude implicitly encodes noise. Drop-in for standard diffusion samplers, but enables faster adaptive/denoising variants. On QM9, 100% validity at 256 NFE and >50% at just 6 NFE. Interesting direction: physics-style training, generative-model inference.

### Modeling phase separation in Si–C–N–H polymer-derived ceramics
_Mortier, Cadars, Masson, et al._ · [arXiv:2605.20358](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.20358) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

MLIP trained on ~9000 Si-C-N-H configurations (amorphous, hot, surfaces, predicted crystals) enables 8000-atom MD that captures graphene-like sheet nucleation from the amorphous SiCN matrix during thermal processing, with 5/7-ring intermediates relaxing into hexagons. Pair distribution functions match experiment closely. Demonstrates that MLIP MD is now the natural tool for "amorphous + heterogeneous + reactive" regimes that defeated classical force fields.

### Octahedral tilting in halide double perovskites
_Baskurt, Fransson, Lindvik, Erhart, Wiktor_ · [arXiv:2605.19414](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19414) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Across 57 HDPs, 0 K DFT plus MLIP MD at 350 K gives a unified story: tilt magnitude and tilt-phase stability track the Goldschmidt tolerance factor, and lone-pair chemistry mostly correlates with tilting because lone-pair cations sit at ionic sizes that shift $t$ — i.e., not the direct cause. Useful counter-narrative against attributing tilting too eagerly to lone-pair stereochemistry.

### Thermal transport in defective uranium nitride
_Chen, Khafizov, Hua, Hurley, Jin_ · [arXiv:2605.17726](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.17726) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Combines an MLIP with Green–Kubo and normal-mode analysis from 300–1500 K. Four-phonon scattering is *essential and sufficient* for high-T anharmonicity in pristine UN. Among point defects, U interstitials dominate phonon-conductivity degradation through broad-spectrum scattering; electron contributions take over above 600 K. A solid example of MLIP-enabled multi-scale thermal-transport workflows for nuclear materials.

### Atomistic modeling of chemical disorder (review)
_Peng, Zhong_ · [arXiv:2605.19124](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19124) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Review of how to bridge the experiment–simulation representation gap for chemically disordered materials, weighing cluster expansion, SQS, Monte Carlo, universal MLIPs, and generative models. Argues for "disorder-native" AI workflows that treat partial occupancy as a controllable variable rather than a nuisance. Worth a read if you build pipelines for alloys, ceramics, or compositionally complex oxides.

## Trends & Themes

- **Foundation-MLIP-as-starting-point is now standard.** Three of this week's applied papers (NMC811, entropy AL, polymer ceramics) explicitly bootstrap from a pretrained MLIP and add a small AL or fine-tuning loop — not a from-scratch MLIP in sight.
- **Mixture-of-Experts arrives in MLIPs.** mlip v2's eSEN is the first major framework shipping MoE for atomistic models. Expect a wave of "MoE-X" papers.
- **Multi-fidelity (DFT → QMC) is becoming practical.** The MACE readout-only fine-tune approach is cheap enough to be a default top-up rather than a heroic effort.
- **Algebraic alternatives to architectural equivariance.** The $\star_G$ work is the most provocative theory piece in a while — provably optimal, interpretable, and able to *discover* the right symmetry from data. Probably won't displace ENNs for force prediction, but it will show up as an analysis tool.
