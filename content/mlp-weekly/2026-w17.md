_The first regular weekly issue. Quiet week on architecture, but two pieces stand out: a uMLIP that calibrates against **experiment** rather than DFT, and an experimental anchor showing where long-range MLPs still fail. Plus a fast bespoke InP dislocation model, NN-potentials landing in GROMACS, and a clever free-energy-learning trick for crystal structure prediction._

## Highlights

- **PET-UAFD** trains an ensemble against multiple electronic-structure references, then calibrates against experimental cohesive energies, lattice constants, and bulk moduli — the first uMLIP to systematically include electronic-structure-method bias in its uncertainty estimates.
- **Silica MRO benchmark** (neutron + X-ray diffraction): even the long-range MACE extension still misses the experimental medium-range order in glassy SiO₂ after quenching. Concrete experimental anchor for the locality-vs-LR debate.
- **Bespoke MACE for InP dislocations** beats the MACE-MPA foundation model by 4× in formation-energy error while running 5× faster — a useful data point on when the foundation-model premium is worth it.

## Papers

### Errors That Matter: Uncertainty-Aware Universal Machine-Learning Potentials Calibrated on Experiments
_Filippo Bigi, Sanggyu Chong, Michele Ceriotti_ · [arXiv:2604.24607](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24607) · physics.chem-ph
PET-UAFD is an ensemble of ML potentials trained against multiple electronic-structure references and then calibrated to experimental cohesive energies, atomization energies, lattice constants, and bulk moduli. The headline contribution is honest UQ that includes the bias from the underlying DFT functional, not just regression error against it.

### Enhancing Molecular Dynamics with Equivariant Machine-Learned Densities
_DenSNet authors_ · [arXiv:2604.24563](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24563) · physics.chem-ph
DenSNet learns the Hohenberg–Kohn map from nuclei to ground-state electron density via an SE(3)-equivariant net (with Δ-learning from superposed atomic densities), then maps density → energy with a second equivariant net. Recovers IR spectra of ethanol/ethanethiol/resorcinol from ML trajectories that match gas-phase experiment — a path to dipoles, polarizabilities, and other electronic observables that vanilla MLIPs can't touch.

### Neutron and X-ray Diffraction Reveal the Limits of Long-Range MLPs for Medium-Range Order in Silica Glass
_Authors et al._ · [arXiv:2604.21222](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21222) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci
Tests short-range and reciprocal-space-gated long-range MACE models against neutron + X-ray scattering on silica glass. The SR model over-structures the network and inflates the first sharp diffraction peak; the LR model fixes the liquid but still misses the experimental amorphous MRO after quenching. The locality fix isn't sufficient — quench dynamics matter.

### Accurate and Efficient Interatomic Potentials for Dislocations in InP
_Authors et al._ · [arXiv:2604.20515](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20515) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci
ACE and MACE models on a new RSCAN DFT dataset, validated against partial-dislocation formation energies. Errors of ≤4% vs 18% for MACE-MPA and 42–50% for prior bespoke potentials — and the bespoke MACE is ~5× faster than MPA at inference. A clean argument that task-specific models still beat foundation models when the budget permits.

### Crystal Structure Prediction with Nuclear Quantum and Finite-Temperature Effects via Deep Free Energy Learning
_Authors et al._ · [arXiv:2604.20230](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20230) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci
Trains a neural network to learn the **SCHA free energy surface** directly (not just the PES), letting CSP runs evaluate finite-T and nuclear-quantum-corrected free energies in a single forward pass. Applied to La-Sc-H at 200 GPa: reproduces LaH₁₀, LaSc₂H₂₄, and predicts a new clathrate hydride P4/mmm LaScH₈, at ~10⁶× lower cost than DFT-SSCHA. Smart reuse of the MLIP toolchain for a free-energy target.

### Enabling Biomolecular Simulations with Neural Network Potentials in GROMACS
_Authors et al._ · [arXiv:2604.21441](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21441) · physics.comp-ph
A flexible ML/MM interface in GROMACS that lets PyTorch-trained NN potentials drive selected subsets or whole systems alongside the existing classical infrastructure. Architecture-agnostic (descriptor-based or message-passing) and integrates with GROMACS's enhanced sampling and free-energy machinery. The kind of plumbing paper that quietly enables a lot of downstream work.

### Monomeric Neural Network Potential for General Covalent Molecules: Linear Alkanes as an Example
_Authors et al._ · [J. Chem. Theory Comput.](https://doi.org/10.1021/acs.jctc.6c00290) · DOI 10.1021/acs.jctc.6c00290
A monomer-decomposed NN potential for general covalent molecules, demonstrated on linear alkanes — extends the per-monomer / many-body decomposition tradition to flexible learnable monomer terms. The Crossref record lacks an abstract; the [arXiv preprint](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.14022) has the full method writeup.

### Autonomous Fabrication of Tailored Defect Structures in 2D Materials Using ML-Enabled STEM
_Authors et al._ · [npj Computational Materials](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41524-026-02025-w) · DOI 10.1038/s41524-026-02025-w
A closed-loop autonomous workflow that uses a CNN + random-forest stack on HAADF imaging to identify atoms in MoS₂, then steers a focused electron beam to selectively eject sulfur and fabricate tailored MoS-nanowire edge structures. Not an MLIP paper, but in the same neighborhood: ML-driven atomistic-scale automation that pairs naturally with potential-driven simulation.

## Trends & Themes

This week's quiet but consistent signal: the field is increasingly testing MLIPs against **experiments**, not just DFT — PET-UAFD calibrates UQ on experiment, the silica paper anchors LR-MLP failure modes in neutron/X-ray data, and the InP work compares directly to literature potentials on a meaningful task. Two months ago we were still mostly DFT-vs-DFT; the bar is rising.
