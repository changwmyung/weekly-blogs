_A heavy week on MACE: benchmarks against magnetism in Fe–Ni, crystal-structure prediction at DFT accuracy, and ferroelectric switching in BaTiO3. Also notable: a reproducibility audit of revPBE-D3 liquid water, and active learning of magnetic MTPs._

## Highlights

- **Reproducibility check on revPBE-D3 water** finds >20% spread in diffusion across published studies — a wake-up call for "standard" AIMD baselines that MLIP training data inherits.
- **MACE under stress test for Fe–Ni magnetism**: a system-specific model nails energies/forces but exposes where universal MACE foundation variants still misbehave on magnetic phase stability.
- **CSP-MACE-Å**: a decomposed (intra + inter + dispersion + Δ-learning) MACE pipeline that aims to displace DFT in molecular crystal structure prediction.

## Papers

### How reproducible are first-principles simulations of liquid water?
_Niamh O'Neill, Benjamin X. Shi, William J. Baldwin, Albert P. Bartók_ · [arXiv:2605.28798](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28798v1) · physics.chem-ph

Multiple published revPBE-D3 AIMD studies of liquid water disagree by >20% on diffusion and >10% on density. The authors use long MLIP-driven MD to argue the discrepancy is largely a sampling/setup issue, not the functional itself. Sobering for anyone training MLIPs on short legacy AIMD trajectories — the reference data has more variance than people quote.

### Can MACE Potentials Accurately Describe Magnetism and Phase Stability in Fe-Ni Alloys? A Systematic Benchmark
_Kushal Ramakrishna, Mani Lokamani, Attila Cangi_ · [arXiv:2605.28395](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28395v1) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Spin-polarized PBE SQS dataset across Fe–Ni compositions; a bespoke MACE-sqs hits 2.0 meV/atom energy error and 24 meV/Å force error. The interesting bit is the comparison against universal/foundation MACE variants, which struggle on magnetic phase stability — confirming that "foundation MLIP" claims need explicit magnetic benchmarks before alloy practitioners trust them.

### DFT Accuracy on Crystal Structure Prediction with Machine Learning Interatomic Potentials
_Laurence I. Midgley, Chen Lin, J. Harry Moore, Flaviano Della Pia, et al._ · [arXiv:2605.28905](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28905v1) · physics.chem-ph

CSP-MACE-Å splits total energy into intramolecular MACE-POLAR (trained on OMol25), an intermolecular MACE-POLAR term, an XDM-style long-range dispersion correction, and a learned Δ-model to reproduce B86bPBE-XDM intermolecular energies. Targeted at molecular crystal structure prediction, where pure foundation MLIPs have historically been too noisy on conformational + lattice energetics. The decomposition is the right idea; whether the Δ-model generalizes off-distribution is the real question.

### Transition from Homogeneous to Domain-Wall-Mediated Polarization Switching in BaTiO3: A Machine-Learning Molecular Dynamics Study
_Po-Yen Chen, Teruyasu Mizoguchi_ · [arXiv:2605.25485](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.25485v1) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

MACEField MLMD on BaTiO3 across supercell sizes reveals a sharp crossover from homogeneous to domain-wall-mediated 180° polarization switching, with a >50% jump in coercive field. Shannon-entropy analysis ties the transition to size-dependent polarization fluctuations. Nice demonstration that MLIP-MD at scale resolves mechanism questions that finite DFT cells simply cannot pose.

### Synthesizability, hardness, and stacking order in multicomponent transition metal carbides from machine-learned potentials
_Xin Liu, Anirudh Raju Natarajan_ · [arXiv:2605.29482](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.29482v1) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Fine-tuned MACE on ~28k DFT calcs covering groups 4–6 transition metals + C; ~10 meV/atom formation-energy error on relevant structures using just 20% of the training set. Used to map synthesizability and elastic-constant landscapes across the multicomponent carbide space — a clean example of fine-tuning a universal MLIP as a high-throughput screen, not an end model.

### Universal Stability of Ga Split Vacancies across α-, β-, and κ-Ga2O3 Polymorphs
_Mohamed Abdelilah Fadla, Myrta Grüning, Lorenzo Stella_ · [arXiv:2605.28743](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28743v1) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

MLIPs sweep non-local defect reconstructions cheaply; HSE06 then nails the energetics. They find split Ga vacancies are universally stable across α, β, κ phases — overturning the assumption that this defect motif is β-specific. Methodologically vanilla but a good template for MLIP-accelerated defect surveys.

### Active learning of collinear magnetic Moment Tensor Potentials (spin-MLIP) — Fe-Pd
_Arseniy Burov, Alexey S. Kotykhov, Dmitry A. Aksyonov, Ivan S. Novikov_ · [arXiv:2605.26897](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26897v1) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Workflow for active learning of magnetic MTPs during MD, using soft-constrained spin-polarized DFT for the reference set. Demonstrated on Fe–Pd. Complements the MACE-magnetism work above — MTPs remain the workhorse for treating spin DOFs explicitly, and this pipeline lowers the cost of generating the constrained-DFT data.

### Charting the thermodynamic stability of hybrid perovskite alloys with machine learning
_Jarno Laakso, Armi Tiihonen, Patrick Rinke_ · [arXiv:2605.30012](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30012v1) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Two-level ML strategy for (Cs/FA)Pb(Br/I)3 and (Cs/FA)Sn(Br/I)3: GNN interatomic potentials for relaxations, plus secondary ML models for direct energy prediction. Quaternary alloy phase diagrams without combinatorial DFT. Practical recipe, not a methodological surprise — but useful for the perovskite PV crowd.

### Finite Temperature Stacking Fault Stability in CoCrNi beyond the Harmonic Approximation
_Reza Namakian, Fei Shuang, Thomas D. Swinburne, Poulumi Dey_ · [arXiv:2605.26364](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26364v1) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Computes T-dependent generalized stacking-fault free energies for random and locally ordered CoCrNi with near-quantum accuracy via MLIPs, going beyond the harmonic approximation. Helps adjudicate the long-running "negative 0 K ISFE" puzzle in this medium-entropy alloy — they find both finite-T and LCO contribute, rather than either alone.

### Search for high-pressure phases of yttrium via a data assimilation approach
_Yuuki Kubo, Takahiro Ishikawa, Yuta Tanaka, Yuki Nakamoto_ · [arXiv:2605.28171](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28171v1) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Combines high-resolution powder XRD with MLIP-driven structure search (up to 128-atom supercells) to revisit the distorted fcc phase of Y. Identifies I4₁/a, Ibam, and R-3 as low-enthalpy candidates and finds no structure relaxes into the previously proposed R-3m. A useful reminder that experiment-in-the-loop structure search benefits as much from cheap MLIP energies as it does from XRD priors.

### Rapid estimation of synthesizability windows of inorganic materials from first principles
_Finja Tadge, Javier Sanz Rodrigo, Andrea Crovetto_ · [arXiv:2605.27162](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.27162v1) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

High-throughput phase-predominance diagrams as a function of T and reactant partial pressures, bridging convex-hull stability and explicit phonon-based phase diagrams. Less MLIP-centric than the others, but the workflow is exactly the kind of thing that becomes tractable once MLIP-quality energies are plug-in cheap.

## Trends & Themes

- **MACE everywhere.** Five of this week's picks lean on MACE variants (foundation, MACE-POLAR, MACEField, fine-tuned task-specific). The architecture is clearly the default substrate; the interesting research is now in what you wrap around it (Δ-models, dispersion corrections, magnetic DOFs).
- **Foundation-model audits sharpen.** The Fe–Ni magnetism benchmark and the revPBE-D3 reproducibility study both push back on uncritical use of "universal" potentials and standard reference data. Healthy.
- **Magnetism is having a moment.** Two independent magnetic-MLIP efforts (MACE Fe–Ni; spin-MLIP MTPs on Fe–Pd) landed the same week — the field is finally past the "spin DOF is too hard" excuse.
- **MLIP-accelerated structure / defect search** continues to be the highest-leverage practical use: Ga2O3 vacancies, yttrium high-P phases, multicomponent carbides. The novelty is no longer the method — it's the question being asked.
