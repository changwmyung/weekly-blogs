_A heavy week for the MLP/MLIP stack itself: faster equivariant kernels, force-aware active learning, motif memory for foundation models, and two pointed audits of where today's foundation atomistic models break (Earth's core iron, density-driven DFT errors). Also a couple of nice "MLIP-as-a-tool" application studies and an LLM-agent take on building MLIPs end-to-end._

## Highlights

- **Bochkarev et al.** push contracted Clebsch–Gordan tensor products in $O(3)$-equivariant MLIPs to $\mathcal{O}(L^3)$ with empirical $L^2$ wall-clock in the practical $l_{\max}$ range — a real lever for ACE-style and message-passing equivariant potentials.
- **Wan et al.** benchmark 17 foundation atomistic models on Earth's core iron and find none reliably reproduce hcp/bcc stability, phonons, *and* multi-component liquid structure — concrete evidence that "foundation" ≠ "transferable" at extreme conditions.
- **Varga-Umbrich et al.** extend Neural Tangent Kernels to a *force-aware* setting and use them for linearly scaling active learning on OC20, beating committee baselines under distribution shift.

## Papers

### Fast contracted Clebsch–Gordan tensor products for equivariant graph neural networks
_Bochkarev, Lysogorskiy, Drautz_ · [arXiv:2605.15073](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15073) · physics.comp-ph

An $\mathcal{O}(L^3)$ algorithm for contracted CG products at fixed CP rank, by mapping the angular integral to a Gauss–Legendre × Fourier grid so radial channel contractions decouple from angular transforms. The parity-odd channels — invisible to a naïve pointwise product on a scalar $S^2$ grid — are recovered via the spherical Poisson bracket $\hat r \cdot [\nabla_{S^2} A \times \nabla_{S^2} B]$. Benchmarks scale empirically as $L^2$ for practical $l_{\max}$ and bend toward $L^3$ at large $l_{\max}$. Directly relevant to ACE/MACE-style architectures where CG costs dominate.

### Assessing foundational atomistic models for iron alloys under Earth's core conditions
_Wan, Wei, Wu, Wentzcovitch, Sun_ · [arXiv:2605.13594](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13594) · physics.geo-ph

Static EOS of hcp and bcc Fe from 17 foundation atomistic models, benchmarked against DFT, with MatterSim and MACE then pushed to phonons, liquid structure, and melting. MACE substantially overestimates bcc stability and underdescribes hcp; both models capture some properties but neither passes the full first-principles battery. The authors flag the missing treatment of thermal electronic excitations as a probable culprit — a useful, specific failure mode rather than a generic "needs more data" complaint.

### Force-Aware Neural Tangent Kernels for Scalable and Robust Active Learning of MLIPs
_Varga-Umbrich, Weller-Davies, Duckworth, Tilly, Peltre, Surana_ · [arXiv:2605.13788](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13788) · cs.LG

Two contributions: (1) a chunked feature-space posterior-variance shortlister that handles ~200k candidates in hours without materializing the full kernel, and (2) an NTK extended to forces via mixed parameter–coordinate derivatives, giving a force NTK and a joint energy-force NTK. The joint kernel gives the lowest energy and force MAE/RMSE on OC20 across splits, and stays robust under candidate-pool shift where committee methods get noisy. A clean answer to "how do I do AL on a foundation MLIP without bankrupting myself?"

### PaMM: Periodic Motif Memory for Atomistic Models with an Explicit Local-Structure Interface
_Dong_ · [arXiv:2605.13297](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13297) · cs.LG

Augments the UMA eSCN-MD edge encoder with explicit pair $(Z_j,Z_i,b_r)$ and triplet $(Z_j,Z_i,Z_k,b_\theta)$ hash-table lookups. At a matched 10k-step UMA-S/OMAT budget, gate-only PaMM gives the best energy MAE and the affine variant the best force MAE; pair-only, triplet-only, random-bucket, and parameter-matched MLP ablations all weaken, suggesting the win is structural rather than capacity. Modest but honest claim — author explicitly does not claim cross-dataset transfer.

### Functional and Density-Driven Errors in DFT: QMC Benchmarks for Solids
_Aouina, Tancogne-Dejean, Botti_ · [arXiv:2605.14745](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14745) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Uses QMC densities of Si, NaCl, and Cu to cleanly separate functional-driven from density-driven errors in DFT functionals. The interesting finding for MLIP people: error cancellation is wildly material-dependent (63% of functionals cancel in Si, only 18% in Cu), and several 1990s GGAs beat modern meta-GGAs. The closing line — that material-dependent cancellation may compromise transferability of ML potentials trained on a single functional — is the kind of thing worth taking seriously when curating reference data.

### Tangent-Plane Evidential Uncertainty in Active Learning for Magnetic Interatomic Potentials
_Cheng, Yu, Xiang_ · [arXiv:2605.12353](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12353) · physics.comp-ph

Extends $\mathrm{e}^2\mathrm{IP}$ evidential uncertainty to magnetic MLIPs by projecting the spin-force likelihood into the tangent plane orthogonal to the local spin direction — so the model doesn't waste probability mass on the radial spin component that constrained-moment DFT never supervises. On BiFeO$_3$ and CrTe$_2$ the tangent-plane indicator correlates with error and outperforms random sampling on energy, force, and spin-force jointly. Niche but a good example of getting the geometry right.

### Melting Behavior and Phase Stability of CaO from Neural Network Potentials
_Menescardi, de Gironcoli_ · [arXiv:2605.14793](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14793) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Trains a PANNA 2.0 + LATTE-descriptor MLIP on ~12k PBEsol AIMD configurations (solid, liquid, interfaces, voids) and computes the CaO melting curve to 20 GPa with both void-nucleated and two-phase coexistence methods. The two methods give $T_m=3055\pm11$ K (VNM) vs $2847\pm15$ K (TPC) at ambient pressure — a 200 K spread that's a useful reminder that "MLIP-accurate" still leaves real methodology choices on the table. Nice that the overheating ratio is shown to drift with pressure (17%→24%), killing a common fixed-$\eta$ assumption.

### Lang2MLIP: End-to-End Language-to-MLIP Development with Autonomous Agentic Workflows
_Li, Orimo, Charoenphakdee_ · [arXiv:2605.14527](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14527) · cs.LG

A multi-agent LLM framework that takes a natural-language description and runs the whole MLIP build as sequential decision-making: dataset, model, evaluation, log → next action. Demonstrated on an SEI system. The pitch is for non-experts and self-correction; the evidence is preliminary (one system, no head-to-head against a fixed expert pipeline), but it's worth tracking as a sign of where MLIP infrastructure is heading.

### Fast and Accurate Prediction of Lattice Thermal Conductivity via Machine Learning Surrogates
_Wang, Yamazaki, Petersen, Ohnishi, et al._ · [arXiv:2605.11610](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11610) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Benchmarks 15 surrogates for $\kappa_\text{lat}$ on the new Phonix database (6,966 entries with anharmonic phonon properties). MLIP-embedding models interpolate best in-distribution; deep nets like ALiEGNN are more robust on OOD/space-group-disjoint splits. Useful as a methods-comparison reference if you're choosing how to layer ML on top of MLIP-driven phonon calculations.

### Active Learning for Gaussian Process Regression Under Self-Induced Boltzmann Weights
_Qing, Moss, Sachs_ · [arXiv:2605.10654](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.10654) · cs.LG

GP-based active learning when the target distribution is the Boltzmann weight of the function being learned — exactly the PES-modeling regime. Closed-form acquisition (`AB-SID-iVAR`) avoids partition-function estimation; a Thompson-sampling variant is given as a higher-variance Monte Carlo alternative. With convergence guarantees and PES benchmarks. A cleaner theoretical handle on a problem most MLIP-AL practitioners just hack around.

## Trends & Themes

Two recurring threads this week: (1) the equivariant-architecture community is squeezing real constant-factor and asymptotic wins out of CG products, edge encoders, and motif memory rather than chasing new top-level architectures, and (2) the foundation-model story is starting to get audited — both at the physics layer (iron at core conditions, electronic-excitation gaps) and at the data layer (functional vs density-driven DFT error, transferability). Active learning is quietly maturing: NTK-based selection, evidential uncertainty for magnetic supervision, and Boltzmann-weighted GP acquisition are all moves toward AL that knows what the downstream physics actually cares about.
