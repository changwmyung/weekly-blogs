_A genuinely meaty week for MLIPs. Two threads dominate: pushing the accuracy–cost Pareto frontier of foundation potentials (DPA4, distilled CCSD(T) specialists), and finally getting **long-range electrostatics** done properly (differentiable particle-mesh Ewald with learned charges *and* dipoles). On the applications side, MLIPs continue to underwrite anharmonic phonon, melting, growth, and phase-change studies that would have been intractable a few years ago._

## Highlights

- **DPA4** (Wang group) hits a new accuracy–cost Pareto frontier on Matbench Discovery — a 2.76M-parameter model beats a 30M-parameter eSEN baseline with ~43× less training compute.
- **Differentiable PME with learned dipoles** (Guo et al.) finally brings long-range electrostatic *and* polarization response into MLIPs with proper $O(N\log N)$ scaling — trained end-to-end through FFT-space electrostatics without direct charge/dipole supervision.
- **Distillation + CCSD(T) fine-tuning** (Shen et al.) shows knowledge transfer from a universal MLIP teacher reduces high-fidelity data requirements by ~60% for non-covalent interaction surfaces — and the choice of teacher is now a first-class design axis.

## Papers

### DPA4: Pushing the Accuracy-Cost Frontier of Interatomic Potentials with EMFA SO(2) Convolution
_Li, Li, Peng, … Zhang, Wang_ · [arXiv:2606.02419](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02419v2) · physics.chem-ph

DPA4 introduces an SE(3)-equivariant architecture built around an "EMFA" SO(2) convolution — a low-rank edge-node product with multi-focus nonlinearity and envelope-gated attention — plus a Lebedev-grid projection that keeps SO(3) equivariance to machine precision through the nonlinearity. The headline number: DPA4-Air (2.76M params) beats eSEN-30M-MP with ~11× fewer params and ~43× less training compute, while DPA4-Pro tops the Matbench Discovery CPS leaderboard. A `torch.compile`-friendly conservative gradient path gives ~3× wall-clock speedup. The accuracy-per-param efficiency is striking and makes this a strong candidate backbone for the next round of large atomistic pretraining.

### Differentiable Particle-Mesh Ewald with Cartesian Tensor Message Passing for Learning Long-Range Electrostatics and Dipole Response
_Guo, Wang, Zhang, … Sun_ · [arXiv:2606.01598](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.01598v1) · physics.comp-ph

The first MLIP I've seen that does long-range *properly*: a fully differentiable PME framework where both learned charges (from scalar features) and learned atomic dipoles (from equivariant vector features) enter the same particle-mesh solver. The dipolar density uses analytic real-space gradients of Hockney-Eastwood spline weights, so charge–dipole and dipole–dipole forces train end-to-end through FFT electrostatics — *without* direct supervision on charges or dipoles. On molten NaCl the long-range channel gives the lowest force RMSE among tested models. This is the right way to do polarization-aware MLIPs for ionic/polar/interfacial systems, and the $O(N\log N)$ scaling actually matches production MD.

### Non-covalent Interactions at cm⁻¹ Accuracy: Data Efficient Physics-Informed Distillation for Machine Learning Interatomic Potentials
_Shen, Akram, Primeau, Zu, Vogiatzis, Zhang, Del Maestro_ · [arXiv:2606.05127](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05127v1) · physics.chem-ph

Distillation from a pretrained universal MLIP teacher, then CCSD(T) fine-tuning. The claim is that what transfers is not just labels but a *physical prior* on interaction length scales, anisotropy, and the repulsive–dispersive balance. On He–benzene, fine-tuning with 30% of the CCSD(T) data beats direct training on 80% — a 60% high-fidelity compute saving. A SAPT-informed adaptive short/long-range head drops validation MAE from 0.75 to 0.49 cm⁻¹. Most interesting: swapping the MLIP teacher under an identical pipeline changes coronene error by an order of magnitude while leaving larger PAHs stable — concrete evidence that the choice of teacher is a real design axis, not a free parameter.

### Stein Kernelized Molecular Dynamics for Active Learning of Interatomic Potentials
_Zou, Birks, Foster, Marzouk_ · [arXiv:2606.04100](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04100v1) · cs.LG

A stochastic Stein-variational-gradient-descent variant adapted to MD, with asynchronous particle updates and a kernel built from global atomic descriptors. Unlike most enhanced samplers, it asymptotically preserves the Boltzmann distribution, giving a principled balance between configurational diversity and energetic relevance. Demonstrated on a MACE fine-tune for alanine dipeptide with an adaptive stopping criterion that picks non-redundant configurations on the fly. Achieves higher accuracy in fewer iterations than active-learning baselines. Cleaner theoretical footing than most acquisition heuristics in this space.

### Faster Molecular Dynamics with Neural Network Potentials via Distilled Multiple Time-Stepping and Nonconservative Forces
_Gouraud, Cattin, Plé, Adjoua, Lagardère, Piquemal_ · [JCTC](https://doi.org/10.1021/acs.jctc.6c00653) · J. Chem. Theory Comput.

Two tricks to speed up NN-potential MD: distill a cheap surrogate to handle frequent inner timesteps in an r-RESPA-style multi-time-stepping scheme, and allow non-conservative force predictions in the inner loop. The combo lets the expensive teacher only be called at the outer step. The non-conservative shortcut is controversial in principle but they've done it carefully — worth reading for groups running production MD where wall-clock is the bottleneck.

### SLUSCHI-UP: A Web Infrastructure for SLUSCHI Melting-Temperature Calculations Using Universal Machine-Learning Interatomic Potentials
_Hong_ · [arXiv:2606.04973](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04973v1) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Web service that couples SLUSCHI (small-cell solid–liquid coexistence) to selectable uMLIPs — currently mace-mpa-0-medium, Allegro-OAM-L, and DPA-3.2-5M-OMat24, with async GPU execution. On MeltBench-10, raw coexistence MAEs sit at 178–327 K depending on backend; PBE-corrected Allegro-OAM-L hits ~166 K MAE across the broader 119-entry snapshot. Author is appropriately humble that these are screening-grade numbers, not a definitive uMLIP ranking. Useful as both a tool and a benchmarking surface for the community.

### Anharmonic lattice dynamics and superconductivity in strained bulk and surface niobium
_Sahoo, Lucrezi, Nunes Ferreira, … Hussein, Heil_ · [arXiv:2606.02730](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02730v1) · cond-mat.supr-con

Nb-specific MLIPs trained on bulk + slab DFT configurations are used to accelerate SSCHA calculations of anharmonically renormalized phonons, which are then combined with DFPT electron–phonon matrix elements. Harmonic phonons exhibit imaginary modes on Nb(001)/(110)/(111) — anharmonicity is *essential*, and ML potentials make the SSCHA loop affordable. Result: tensile strain pushes bulk Nb's $T_c$ from 9.5 K to 14.5 K at 6% expansion; Nb(001) is the best of the three low-index surfaces at 10.0 K. A nice instance of MLIPs unlocking a calculation that would otherwise be prohibitive.

### Data-driven mapping of borophene growth pathways
_Bousige, Furstoss, Lam, Mignon_ · [arXiv:2606.03886](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03886v1) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

A reactive MLIP plus grand-canonical Monte Carlo plus data-driven structural classification, used to map borophene nucleation and growth on Ag(111) and Ag(100). Reproduces the experimentally observed prevalence of $\beta_{12}/\chi_3$ phases and their temperature-dependent competition, and identifies kinetic pathways from metastable nuclei to long-range order. Concrete synthesis-window predictions come out of it. A good template for how to do reactive MLIP + sampling for 2D-material polymorph selection.

### Unconventional crystallization pathway bypassing the intermediate cubic phase in phase-change superlattices
_Wang, Chen, Wang, … Zhang, Li_ · [arXiv:2606.03309](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03309v1) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Large-scale MD with an MLIP applied to the long-standing question of how partially amorphized GST superlattices recrystallize. They show residual crystalline regions nucleate direct recrystallization into the superlattice phase, bypassing the cubic intermediate, and the recovered phase is a structurally ordered but chemically disordered "defective superlattice." That defective region is also the most melt-prone, making it the natural active region of a PCM cell. Concrete mechanistic answer to a debated question, only possible at this scale because of the MLIP.

### Role of Characteristic Length Scale in Interface Graphitization-Induced Wear Resistance of Diamond and Amorphous Carbon
_Shi, Zhang, Liao, Xu, Wu, Zhong, Chang_ · [arXiv:2606.03325](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03325v1) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Trains a carbon MLIP and runs MD of diamond/diamond and amorphous/amorphous carbon sliding contacts. Finds that diamond interfaces grow a continuous sp² reconstruction layer of 30–45 Å, while amorphous carbon only develops isolated sp² patches of 8–12 Å — and this length-scale gap directly controls the density of weakly bonded interfacial atoms and therefore wear rate. Leads to a concrete design suggestion: protect (111) close-packed planes in diamond coatings to regulate friction-induced graphitization.

### Quantum annealing for materials
_Fiorentino, Marzari_ · [arXiv:2606.03405](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03405v1) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Path-integral MD reformulation of quantum annealing for materials-science global optimization. Works with either empirical force fields or MLIPs, and can either be used as a pure PES optimizer or as a quantum-informed structure searcher that bakes in nuclear quantum effects from the start — particularly relevant for high-pressure hydrides. Not a new MLIP, but a useful new consumer of them.

## Trends & Themes

Two clear directions this week:

1. **Foundation-model maturation.** DPA4's accuracy-per-param numbers and the distillation paper's "teacher choice is a design axis" framing both point at the same thing: the community is past the "scale it up" phase and is now hunting for *informative* priors and efficient architectures. Expect more papers like this through the year.
2. **Long-range physics, properly handled.** The differentiable PME paper is, I think, the most important methodological result of the week. Most MLIPs still cut off at 5–6 Å and quietly hope nothing important is happening past that; this gives a clean, scalable way to learn charge *and* dipole response with the right asymptotics, and it slots into production MD without an $O(N^2)$ Ewald sum bottleneck.

Honorable mention on the applications side: it's striking how many of this week's condensed-matter papers (Nb superconductivity, GST phase-change, borophene growth, diamond tribology) treat MLIPs as a settled tool — the novelty is the *physics*, not the potential. That's a healthy sign for the field.
