_A practitioner-heavy week: fine-tuning recipes for MLIP foundation models, a cautionary tale about PBE-based foundation models getting HfO₂'s ground state wrong, and an agentic harness aimed squarely at the "human bottleneck" now that universal potentials have eaten the compute bottleneck._

## Highlights

- **Tompa et al.** systematically benchmark seven fine-tuning strategies for MACE-family foundation models — and find that getting E₀ and hyperparameters right matters more than the strategy itself, with multihead replay the only approach that consistently preserves OOD robustness.
- **Tang et al.** show that PBE-based foundation models (NequIP-OAM-L, MatterSim-v1-5M) wrongly predict an I4₁/amd ground state for HfO₂ — a functional-level error, not a learning failure, and a warning for anyone trusting foundation-model phase diagrams.
- **Park et al. (SNU/Han group)** release Paimon, an agent harness for atomistic simulations that targets the new bottleneck — silent errors and prep/analysis time — rather than the old one.

## Papers

### Fine-tuning MLIP foundation models: strategies for accuracy and transferability
_Tompa, Varga-Umbrich, Batatia, Elena, Bernstein, Csányi_ · [arXiv:2606.12704](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12704) · physics.chem-ph

Seven fine-tuning strategies (naive, two layer-freezing variants, LoRA, multihead replay, pseudolabelled replay, replay+LoRA) tested across five chemistries (aqueous NaCl, ice, S$_\mathrm{N}$2, SPICE biomolecules, Li electrolytes), three MACE foundation-model generations, and training-set sizes spanning five orders of magnitude. The headline finding is sobering for the community: once foundation-model quality, atomic reference energies, and hyperparameters are correct, the choice of strategy is largely a wash for in-distribution accuracy. Multihead replay (with original or pseudolabelled data) is singled out as the only tested approach that reliably preserves pretraining-distribution accuracy and short-range repulsion — i.e., the only one that won't quietly break when you ship the model. LoRA for equivariant message-passing layers and pseudolabelled replay are added to MACE.

### A wrong ground-state structure of HfO₂ predicted by machine-learning interatomic potentials based on the PBE functional
_Tang, Wei, Wang, Zhou, Zhang, Huang, Chen_ · [arXiv:2606.12811](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12811) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

A clean, well-aimed shot at a comfortable assumption: a PBE-trained MLIP for HfO₂ finds an I4₁/amd phase _lower in energy_ than the experimentally established monoclinic P2₁/c ground state. Crucially, the same wrong prediction comes out of widely used foundation models — NequIP-OAM-L and MatterSim-v1-5M — so this is not a fitting bug but a faithful reproduction of PBE's bias toward low-density sixfold-coordinated Hf-O units. PBEsol and LDA largely fix it. The implication is uncomfortable: any structure search, ferroelectric switching barrier, or phase-diagram screen built on PBE-pretrained foundation MLIPs inherits this error wherever low-density octahedral motifs are competitive.

### A Robust Agentic Framework for Expert-Level Automation of Atomistic Simulations
_Park, Chung, You, Kim, Ju, Han_ · [arXiv:2606.09422](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09422) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Seungwu Han's group introduces **Paimon**, an agent harness that frames the post-uMLIP bottleneck as a human one: input prep, sanity-checking, analysis. The interesting claim is the explicit focus on _suppressing silent errors_ — physically wrong but plausible-looking outputs — rather than just chaining tools. Demonstrated on liquid-electrolyte workflows and shown cooperating with an external scientific agent to reproduce a literature methodology autonomously. Worth watching as a reference design for how MLIP workflows get productized.

### Approaching the Limit of Intrinsic Crystalline Thermal Insulation
_Cheng, Cui, Jayaraman, Ji, Wang, Zeng, Levinsky, Hejtmanek, Candolfi, Guilmeau, Shen, Chen_ · [arXiv:2606.11072](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11072) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

uMLIP + high-fidelity phonon transport theory used as a high-throughput screen for ultralow-κ crystals, identifying dozens of candidates below 0.2 W m⁻¹ K⁻¹ at 300 K. They report **CsTlI₄ at 0.14 W m⁻¹ K⁻¹**, experimentally validated — a record for crystalline materials at room temperature. The structural rationale (hierarchical Cs-I + antibonding Tl-I bonding, suppressing both particle-like and wave-like phonon transport) is recovered through interpretable IFC-based descriptors. A nice case study of MLIP screening that actually closes the loop with synthesis.

### Robust AI-Driven Discovery of Electronic Metal Phosphide Semiconductors
_Zhu, Faizan, Li, Li, Ren, Xie, Zhang_ · [arXiv:2606.10251](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10251) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

A generative-plus-MLIP discovery pipeline: ICSD Wyckoff-site substitution and MatterGen for proposing structures, a domain-finetuned **DPA3** potential for stability prescreening, then HSE06 for validation. Yields 3,574 previously unreported stable phosphides and 196 semiconductors with HSE gaps in 0–3 eV, of which 15 newly discovered phosphides are flagged as promising for optoelectronics or thermoelectrics. The DPA3 prescreen is doing the real work — without it the DFT bill for that volume of MatterGen output is unaffordable.

### Cepstral Analysis to accelerate Green-Kubo thermal conductivity calculations of Metal-Organic Frameworks
_Lindner, Zojer, Wieser_ · [arXiv:2606.13588](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13588) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Green-Kubo for MOFs is notoriously noisy and parameter-sensitive. The authors pair MTP MLIPs with cepstral analysis on MOF-5, HKUST-1, and ZIF-8, getting stable κ estimates that converge in 1–2 ns of total sampling and don't depend on hand-tuned correlation windows. The MLIP part is unsurprising at this point; the real contribution is making the post-processing reproducible and automation-friendly. Useful template for anyone doing MLIP-based transport in low-κ frameworks.

### Precipitate phase selection and grain boundary morphology in Cu-Ni-Si-Mn alloys: A machine-learning interatomic potential study
_Wani, Jeong, Jeon, Kim, Park, Choi, Han, Han, Ryu_ · [arXiv:2606.09023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09023) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Industrially-relevant question — why does Mn addition change Cu-Ni-Si grain-boundary precipitates from irregular Ni₂Si to film-like G-phase (Mn₆Ni₁₆Si₇), with corresponding mechanical-property improvements? — answered with MLIP-driven interface energetics at DFT accuracy. The atomic-scale picture (coherent vs. incoherent Cu/precipitate interfaces, repulsive open-boundary regions for Ni₂Si vs. moderate-energy incoherent G-phase interfaces) explains the morphology shift and gives an actionable handle for alloy design. Good example of MLIP enabling interface calculations that DFT alone couldn't reach at scale.

### Bi-S network origin of cation-disorder stability and dispersive band edges in AgBiS₂
_Liang, Geng, Kang, Qiu, Yao, Li, Zhang, Sun, Chen, Zhang, Wei, Guan_ · [arXiv:2606.09704](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09704) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Combines an MLIP for structural sampling with a deep-learning Hamiltonian (DeepH-style) for the electronic structure to resolve the long-debated ordered structure and disorder-stability of AgBiS₂. The picture that drops out: a connected 3D Bi-S network stabilizes the rocksalt-like disordered phase and preserves dispersive conduction-band edges even under strong cation disorder, while mobile Ag localizes the valence states. Useful as a methodological template — MLIP + DL-Hamiltonian is starting to look like a viable replacement for direct DFT on disordered semiconductor alloys.

### k-Means Clustering in Fingerprint-Based Configuration Selection for Fitting Interatomic Potentials
_Lebeda, Drahokoupil, Löbel, Vlčák_ · [arXiv:2606.09575](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09575) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Small but useful: k-means clustering on CrystalNN + RDF fingerprints, applied to selecting training configurations for an EAM Ti potential. Beats random selection at small set sizes and suggests ~30 configurations suffice for the full 1800-configuration accuracy. The interesting bit is the t-SNE diagnostic showing redundancy between vacancy and non-vacancy configurations — k-means catches it, random doesn't. Demonstrated on EAM, but the recipe transfers cleanly to MLIPs where training-set curation is the main human cost.

### Magnetic HIP-NN for spin dynamics in disordered itinerant magnets
_Ghosh, Fan, Zhang, Barros, Chern_ · [arXiv:2606.10349](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10349) · cond-mat.dis-nn

Extends HIP-NN to spin variables while preserving spin-rotation symmetry, enabling LLG dynamics on disordered s-d exchange models that would be intractable via exact diagonalization. Reproduces local torques and nonequilibrium spatial spin correlations after thermal quenches. Worth flagging because the framework is differentiable in both atomic coordinates and spins, which is the foundation for properly coupled atom-spin potentials — a niche but growing direction for MLIPs in magnetic materials.

## Trends & Themes

The community is visibly maturing past "can we train a potential?" toward "can we trust and operationalize one?". Three converging signals this week: (1) Tompa et al. providing the first systematic fine-tuning recipe book for foundation models; (2) Tang et al. exposing that the _underlying functional_ is now the dominant error source, not the ML fit; (3) Park et al. building tooling that treats _silent errors_ as the headline risk. Meanwhile foundation-MLIP + downstream physics (phonons, electronic-structure DL Hamiltonians, generative discovery) is becoming a default rather than a novelty — see CsTlI₄ and the phosphide screen for clean examples.
