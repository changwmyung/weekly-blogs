_A week dominated by attempts to make MLIPs more honest about what they don't know: experiment-anchored uncertainty, sober benchmarks of "universal" models on hot MOFs, and structural priors (fragment charge conservation, density-first learning) that put physics back into the loss._

## Highlights

- **PET-UAFD** calibrates an ensemble of universal MLIPs against experimental cohesive energies, lattice constants, and bulk moduli — turning MLIPs from "faithful interpolators of DFT" into uncertainty-aware predictors anchored in measurements.
- A new **high-temperature MOF benchmark** (300/1000/2000 K AIMD on nine Zn/Zr frameworks) shows ORB-v3 and fairchem OMAT lead the pack, but _all_ uMLIPs degrade sharply at 2000 K — generative trajectory error far exceeds the static validation loss.
- A Bayesian-active-learning + SSCHA framework predicts the CsPbI₃ α→δ degradation transition from only **a few tens to a few hundred** total-energy calculations, suggesting most anharmonic phase-diagram problems may be over-sampled.

## Papers

### Errors that matter: Uncertainty-aware universal machine-learning potentials calibrated on experiments
_Kellner, Hansen, Bligaard, Jacobsen, Ceriotti_ · [arXiv:2604.24607](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24607) · physics.chem-ph

Most MLIP uncertainty quantification estimates how far a model is from its DFT reference — but the reference itself is wrong relative to nature. The authors train an ensemble (PET-UAFD) on multiple electronic-structure references and recalibrate it against experimental cohesive energies, atomization energies, lattice constants, and bulk moduli, then validate on the density and structure of liquids. They also propose PET-EXP, a shallow-ensemble + reweighting trick that gives experimental uncertainty estimates at the cost of running a single MLIP. The framing (UAFD-style ensembling carried over from DFT functionals to MLIPs) is the right way to talk about MLIP error, and the liquids transferability is a real test.

### Benchmarking Universal Machine-Learned Interatomic Potentials for High-Temperature Metal-Organic Framework Chemistry
_Edwards, Evans_ · [arXiv:2604.25262](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25262) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

A 40 ps AIMD benchmark at 300, 1000, and 2000 K across nine MOFs (ZIF-8, CALF-20, MOF-5/-10, MIP-206, UiO-66/-67/-66-NH₂, NU-1000) including linker degradation and node aggregation events. Tested: ORB-v3, MACE-MP-0a, MACE-MPA-0, fairchem ODAC23, fairchem OMAT. ORB-v3 and OMAT have the lowest energy/force/stress errors, but long ORB-v3 trajectories accumulate generative error well beyond what static validation suggests. A useful, narrowly-scoped reality check on uMLIP claims of universality — and a dataset others can rebenchmark against.

### Predicting challenging phase transitions with Bayesian active learning
_Bastonero, Joalland, Cignarella, Monacelli, Marzari_ · [arXiv:2604.25756](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25756) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

On-the-fly Bayesian learning of interatomic potentials coupled to the stochastic self-consistent harmonic approximation (SSCHA), targeting strongly anharmonic phase diagrams. Demonstrated on Li₂O and CsPbI₃ with 44, 256, and 50 total-energy calculations — and the framework reproduces the CsPbI₃ α→δ transition (the spontaneous degradation that kills perovskite solar cells) with quantitative accuracy. The headline number isn't the transition temperature, it's the sample efficiency: SSCHA + targeted BAL appears to make first-principles phase-diagram prediction routine.

### Mixture of Experts Framework in Machine Learning Interatomic Potentials for Atomistic Simulations
_de Miranda Nascimento, Descoteaux, Zichi, Tan, Witt, Molinari, Mantha, Kitchaev, Kornbluth, Gadelrab, Tuffile, Kozinsky_ · [arXiv:2604.26143](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26143) · physics.comp-ph

Spatial mixture-of-experts on top of E(3)-equivariant Allegro: a high-capacity model for chemically complex regions (e.g. a Pt/CO interface), a lightweight model for bulk lattice. The hard part — mechanical-mismatch artifacts at the static decomposition boundary — is handled with a co-training penalty enforcing per-atom energy and force agreement on shared bulk environments. Reported >2× speedup on Pt+CO with no loss of energy conservation. Conceptually similar to multifidelity DFT embedding but learned end-to-end; the co-training trick is the contribution worth borrowing.

### Fragment-Constrained Charge Equilibration for Charge-Aware Machine Learning Potentials at Electrochemical Interfaces
_Peeketi, Uberuaga, Jones_ · [arXiv:2604.27910](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27910) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Global charge-equilibration (QEq) layers in charge-aware MLIPs equilibrate electrode and electrolyte at a single chemical potential, which destroys the very gradient that defines the double layer. The authors make fragment identification differentiable so a per-fragment QEq solver can be used in a reactive setting (Soft-FQEq), and bolt it onto HIP-NN. On IrO₂/H₂O/Na⁺/ClO₄⁻ trained against DDEC6 charges, the model recovers an electrode-to-electrolyte potential gradient that collapses to a flat profile when the same network is run with global QEq at inference — a clean ablation that pins the effect on the solver, not the features. The right architectural fix for electrochemistry MLIPs.

### Enhancing molecular dynamics with equivariant machine-learned densities (DenSNet)
_Bogojeski, Hasyim, Vogt-Maranto, Müller, Burke, Tuckerman_ · [arXiv:2604.24563](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24563) · physics.chem-ph

Predicts the ground-state electron density (as coefficients in an atom-centered Gaussian basis) with an SE(3)-equivariant network using superposed atomic densities as a Δ-learning prior, then maps that density to the energy with a second equivariant network. The point isn't density prediction for its own sake — it's that you get dipole moments, polarizabilities, and IR spectra from MD trajectories without separate force-field-style scalar predictors. Validated on ethanol/ethanethiol/resorcinol IR (vs experiment) and extrapolated from 1–6-monomer polythiophenes to 12-monomer chains. A sensible direction for MLIPs that need to talk to spectroscopy.

### VibroML: an automated toolkit for high-throughput vibrational analysis and dynamic instability remediation of crystalline materials using machine-learned potentials
_Almeida Gouvêa, Rignanese_ · [arXiv:2604.27685](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27685) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

Most MLIP-driven phonon screening pipelines stop at "is the structure dynamically stable?" VibroML adds a remediation layer: an energy-guided genetic algorithm (claimed to outperform soft-mode-following) plus an MD step to test finite-temperature retention, and integration with the authors' ProtoCSP for compositional rescue. Demonstrated by stabilizing Cs₂KInI₆ and KTaSe₃ and by mining the Alexandria database for the ~50% of quaternary and 99.5% of quinary stoichiometries currently absent. Whether the GA actually beats soft-mode following _in general_ would need more comparisons, but the open-source workflow is useful as-is.

### Ultrafast Sliding Ferroelectric Switching in Bilayer Hexagonal Boron Nitride Revealed by Deep Learning Molecular Dynamics
_Wang, Chen, Mizoguchi_ · [arXiv:2604.27342](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27342) · cond-mat.mtrl-sci

A fine-tuned MACE potential paired with a separate equivariant GCNN that predicts Born effective charges in real time, used to do field-driven non-equilibrium MD on AB-stacked bilayer h-BN. Combined with a real-space path-integral polarization formalism (with a Gaussian-convolution background subtraction for the dielectric piece) the simulations show coherent rigid-sliding switching in ~5 ps and reproduce hysteresis loops qualitatively matching experiment. Nice example of the "MLIP + auxiliary equivariant property head" pattern that's becoming the way to do polarization-aware MD.

### Towards Accelerated SCF Workflows with Equivariant Density-Matrix Learning and Analytic Refinement (dm-PhiSNet)
_Yescas-Ramos, Álvarez-García, Sauceda_ · [arXiv:2604.27256](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27256) · physics.chem-ph

Predicts one-electron reduced density matrices in an AO basis with PhiSNet, then runs an analytic refinement block that enforces electron-number conservation and pushes toward generalized idempotency in the Löwdin-orthogonalized basis. Used as an SCF initial guess on six small closed-shell systems, it cuts SCF iterations 49–81% and gives reasonable one-shot energies and Hellmann–Feynman forces without any force supervision. Adjacent to MLIPs rather than one of them, but it points at a future where the same equivariant backbone serves both energy prediction and electronic-structure warm-starts.

## Trends & Themes

Three threads ran through the week:

1. **Honesty about error.** PET-UAFD and the MOF benchmark are both arguing that MLIP papers have been measuring the wrong thing — DFT-relative force RMSE rather than experiment-relative observables, and static validation loss rather than long-trajectory generative error. Expect more of this.
2. **Putting physics back in.** Soft-FQEq (fragment charge conservation), DenSNet (density as the central learned object), dm-PhiSNet (idempotency constraints): the field is moving away from pure black-box scalar regression toward enforced electronic-structure invariants.
3. **Sample efficiency via principled priors.** Bayesian active learning + SSCHA hitting CsPbI₃'s phase diagram with ~50 configurations, and DenSNet's superposed-atomic-density Δ-learning prior, both suggest the right inductive bias is worth at least an order of magnitude of training data.
